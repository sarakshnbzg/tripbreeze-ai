# Transit and Layover Rules by Destination

Use these notes as a planning guide for airport transits and layovers, not as a substitute for official guidance. Transit rules depend heavily on the passport you hold, whether you stay airside, whether luggage is through-checked, and the airline's own practices.

- A "self-transfer" — where you collect bags and re-check on a separate ticket — counts as an entry in most countries. Plan for immigration, not just airside transit.
- Transit-without-visa (TWOV) schemes usually require confirmed onward tickets, same-airport transit, short layover windows, and specific airside conditions.
- Schengen countries share a single airside transit area for onward Schengen flights. A stop in a Schengen country followed by a non-Schengen flight requires passing immigration.

## France (Schengen Area)
- **Airside transit (same airport, international-to-international):** Usually allowed without a visa for most nationalities, but an Airport Transit Visa (ATV) is required for certain passports (Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Iran, Iraq, Nigeria, Pakistan, Somalia, Sri Lanka, Syria, and others; list changes).
- **Leaving the airside area:** Requires Schengen entry clearance.
- **Changing airports in Paris (CDG to ORY or vice versa):** Counts as entering Schengen territory — you must be admitted.
- **Self-transfer:** You will reclaim bags landside, which means clearing immigration and customs.

## Japan
- **Airside transit (same airport, international-to-international):** Most nationalities can transit Narita, Haneda, and Kansai airside up to 72 hours without a visa. Bags must be through-checked.
- **Shore Pass:** Lets visa-required nationals enter Japan for up to 72 hours during a transit to a third country. Must be requested at immigration on arrival; not guaranteed.
- **Transit Pass for same-day continuation:** Available for onward travel within the same day.
- **Self-transfer:** You must enter Japan. Use the Shore Pass or a tourist visa-free entry if eligible.

## United States
- **There is no airside international transit in the US.** All travelers entering the US, even to change planes, must clear immigration and customs.
- **Implications:** Travelers whose nationalities require a US visa need one even for a same-day connection (e.g., LAX → MEX via IAH).
- **Pre-clearance airports (e.g., Dublin, Toronto, Shannon):** Clear US immigration before boarding a US-bound flight. Simplifies the arrival process but does not change the visa requirement.
- **Self-transfer:** Expected — all connections require bag recheck after customs.

## Thailand
- **Airside transit (same airport, international-to-international):** Most nationalities can transit Bangkok (BKK or DMK) airside without a Thai visa, up to 12–24 hours depending on airline facilities. Through-checked bags required.
- **Changing airports in Bangkok (BKK ↔ DMK):** Counts as entering Thailand. Use the visa-on-arrival or visa-exempt entry if eligible.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires entry. Budget 4–5 hours between flights on separate tickets.

## Spain (Schengen Area)
- **Airside transit:** Allowed for most nationalities; some require a Schengen Airport Transit Visa (same list as France).
- **Schengen-to-Schengen connections:** Treated as domestic; no border at the onward gate.
- **Schengen-to-non-Schengen connections:** Pass through passport control between airside zones.
- **Self-transfer:** Entry required.

## UAE (Dubai)
- **Airside transit:** Wide airside facilities at DXB and AUH; most nationalities transit without a visa.
- **Transit visa (for leaving airside):** 48-hour transit visa (~AED 50 plus charges) and 96-hour transit visa (~AED 150) available through many airlines. Bookable in advance.
- **Changing terminals (DXB):** Terminals 1, 2, and 3 are landside-connected — changing terminals requires entering the UAE.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires entry; the 96-hour transit visa is often the fastest option for travelers without visa-on-arrival eligibility.

## Italy (Schengen Area)
- **Airside transit:** Allowed for most nationalities; same Schengen ATV list applies.
- **Schengen connections:** No border between flights.
- **Non-Schengen connections:** Passport control between zones.
- **Self-transfer:** Entry required.

## Germany (Schengen Area)
- **Airside transit:** Frankfurt and Munich have large transit zones. Most nationalities transit airside without visa; Schengen ATV list applies.
- **Changing terminals (FRA):** Some terminal changes route travelers through passport control — allow extra time.
- **Self-transfer:** Entry required.

## Netherlands (Schengen Area)
- **Airside transit:** Schiphol has no separate airside transit area — non-Schengen arrivals pass through immigration on arrival even when transiting.
- **Implication:** Travelers who need a Schengen visa generally need one to transit Schiphol, unless they hold a residence permit or visa from another Schengen state.
- **Self-transfer:** Entry required.

## Singapore
- **Airside transit:** Changi Airport has extensive airside transit facilities (Terminals 1, 2, 3, 4 connected via Skytrain or shuttle bus). Most nationalities transit without visa up to 24 hours (and often longer).
- **Free Singapore Tour:** Eligible transit passengers with 5.5+ hour layovers can join a complimentary city tour — requires clearing immigration with a short-term visit pass.
- **Self-transfer:** Possible via the Early Check-In facility at some terminals, but most self-transfer passengers must collect bags and clear immigration.

## India
- **Airside transit:** Allowed at Delhi (DEL), Mumbai (BOM), Bengaluru (BLR), Chennai (MAA), Hyderabad (HYD), and Kolkata (CCU) for most nationalities up to 24 hours, provided bags are through-checked and onward ticket is confirmed.
- **Changing airports or leaving the airport:** Requires a tourist visa, e-Visa, or transit visa (USD 40, up to 15 days).
- **Self-transfer:** Requires an Indian visa.

## Indonesia (Bali)
- **Airside transit:** Denpasar (DPS) and Jakarta (CGK) allow airside transit for most nationalities when bags are through-checked. Short same-terminal connections only.
- **Leaving the airport:** Requires visa on arrival or e-Visa.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires entry; budget time for the visa-on-arrival queue.

## Vietnam
- **Airside transit:** Hanoi (HAN), Ho Chi Minh City (SGN), and Da Nang (DAD) allow airside transit up to 24 hours for most nationalities with through-checked bags.
- **Leaving the airport:** Requires e-Visa or visa on arrival (with pre-approval letter for some nationalities).
- **Self-transfer:** Requires a visa.

## Australia
- **Airside transit:** An Australian visa (including the low-cost Transit Visa, subclass 771) is required by most nationalities even for airside connections at Sydney, Melbourne, and other airports. Citizens of some countries are exempt if transit is under 8 hours and airside.
- **New Zealand:** Australia and New Zealand share special arrangements — but visa rules still apply.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires entry clearance.

## New Zealand
- **Airside transit:** Transit Visa usually required for most non-visa-waiver nationals even for short layovers at Auckland (AKL), although a limited Transit Visa Waiver scheme exists.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires NZeTA or visitor visa.

## Canada
- **Airside transit:** An eTA or Canadian visa is required for most nationalities even to transit airside, unless the traveler qualifies for the Transit Without Visa (TWOV) or China Transit Program — limited to specific cities and US-bound routes.
- **Preclearance for US connections:** US immigration is cleared before boarding at Canadian airports.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires an eTA or visa.

## United Kingdom
- **Airside transit:** Most nationalities can transit airside at Heathrow, Gatwick, Manchester, and other airports without a visa. Certain passports require a Direct Airside Transit Visa (DATV).
- **Landside transit / self-transfer:** Requires a Visitor in Transit visa or an Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) / standard visitor visa depending on nationality.
- **Changing airports in London (LHR ↔ LGW, etc.):** Counts as entering the UK.

## South Africa
- **Airside transit:** Johannesburg (JNB) and Cape Town (CPT) allow airside transit for many nationalities; some require a transit visa.
- **Leaving the airport:** Requires the appropriate visa.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires entry.

## Turkey
- **Airside transit:** Istanbul (IST) allows airside transit for most nationalities with through-checked bags.
- **Stopover program:** Turkish Airlines offers free hotel stays on long layovers via the TourIstanbul program for eligible passengers.
- **Leaving the airport:** Requires e-Visa (many nationalities eligible) or visa on arrival.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires entry clearance.

## Hong Kong (China SAR)
- **Airside transit:** Hong Kong International allows airside transit up to 24 hours for most nationalities.
- **Leaving the airport:** Visa-free entry available to many nationalities for 7, 14, or 90 days.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires entry.
- **Transit to/from mainland China:** Hong Kong and mainland China have separate immigration — a connection from Hong Kong to Beijing is an international transit, not domestic.

## Portugal (Schengen Area)
- **Airside transit:** Lisbon (LIS) and Porto (OPO) allow airside transit for most nationalities; the Schengen Airport Transit Visa list applies to a few passports.
- **Schengen-to-Schengen connections:** Treated as domestic; no border at the onward gate.
- **Non-Schengen connections:** Pass through passport control between airside zones.
- **Self-transfer:** Entry required.
- **Special note:** Lisbon routinely sees long immigration queues; allow extra time for non-EU arrivals connecting to onward flights.

## Greece (Schengen Area)
- **Airside transit:** Athens (ATH) and Thessaloniki (SKG) allow airside transit for most nationalities; Schengen ATV list applies.
- **Schengen connections:** No border between flights.
- **Non-Schengen connections:** Passport control between zones.
- **Island connections:** Athens is the hub for island flights. A connection from an international flight to a domestic island flight means clearing Schengen entry.
- **Self-transfer:** Entry required.

## Ireland
- **Airside transit:** Dublin (DUB), Shannon (SNN), and Cork (ORK) generally allow airside transit for most nationalities with through-checked bags; certain passports require an Irish transit visa.
- **US Preclearance:** Dublin and Shannon host US Customs and Border Protection preclearance facilities. Travelers clear US immigration before boarding; the arrival in the US is treated as domestic. A valid US visa or ESTA is still required.
- **Self-transfer:** Most travelers will need to collect bags and re-check, which requires clearing Irish immigration.
- **Special note:** Ireland is in the Common Travel Area with the UK but is not in Schengen — connections from Schengen countries still pass through passport control.

## Switzerland (Schengen Area)
- **Airside transit:** Zurich (ZRH) and Geneva (GVA) allow airside transit for most nationalities; Schengen ATV list applies.
- **Schengen-to-Schengen connections:** Treated as domestic; no border.
- **Non-Schengen connections:** Passport control between zones.
- **Self-transfer:** Entry required.
- **Special note:** Some travelers route through Geneva specifically because transfers between air and rail are smooth, but the train platform is outside the airside zone — using it requires entering Schengen.

## Norway (Schengen Area)
- **Airside transit:** Oslo (OSL) and Bergen (BGO) allow airside transit for most nationalities; Schengen ATV list applies.
- **Schengen connections:** Treated as domestic; no border.
- **Non-Schengen connections:** Passport control between zones.
- **Self-transfer:** Entry required.
- **Svalbard:** Flights between mainland Norway and Svalbard pass through Schengen exit controls at Oslo (Svalbard is outside Schengen) — expect a passport check in both directions.

## Czech Republic (Schengen Area)
- **Airside transit:** Prague (PRG) allows airside transit for most nationalities; Schengen ATV list applies.
- **Schengen-to-Schengen connections:** Treated as domestic; no border.
- **Non-Schengen connections:** Passport control between airside zones.
- **Self-transfer:** Entry required.

## Argentina
- **Airside transit:** Ezeiza (EZE) and Aeroparque (AEP) allow airside transit for most nationalities with through-checked bags.
- **Changing airports in Buenos Aires (EZE ↔ AEP):** Counts as entering Argentina — most international flights use EZE and most domestic use AEP, so this is a common scenario requiring a visa or visa-free entry.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires entry; allow 4+ hours and time for the cross-city transfer.

## Morocco
- **Airside transit:** Casablanca (CMN) is a Royal Air Maroc hub and allows airside transit for most nationalities with through-checked bags, up to 24 hours.
- **Leaving the airport:** Many nationalities are visa-free for tourism up to 90 days (US, UK, EU, Canada, Australia, among others). Some passports need a visa.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires entry; visa-free travelers can use this route.
- **Special note:** CMN has limited airside facilities for long layovers — airlines often issue complimentary hotel vouchers for layovers over 8 hours.

## Egypt
- **Airside transit:** Cairo (CAI) allows airside transit for most nationalities with through-checked bags, up to 24 hours.
- **Leaving the airport (Cairo Stopover):** EgyptAir and the tourism authority offer visas-on-arrival for layovers as short as 6 hours; some nationalities also qualify for the standard visa-on-arrival.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires an Egyptian visa (e-Visa or visa-on-arrival, depending on nationality).
- **Special note:** Domestic connections to Luxor, Aswan, or Hurghada from an international arrival require entry.

## Philippines
- **Airside transit:** Manila (MNL) and Cebu (CEB) allow airside transit for most nationalities with through-checked bags, up to 72 hours in many cases.
- **Leaving the airport:** Many nationalities (including US, EU, UK, Canada, Japan, Australia) are visa-free for tourism up to 30 days.
- **Changing terminals in Manila (NAIA Terminals 1–4):** Terminals are not airside-connected. Any terminal change requires collecting bags, exiting, and re-entering — effectively a self-transfer.
- **Self-transfer:** Requires entry for most nationalities; budget at least 4 hours for terminal transfers at MNL.
