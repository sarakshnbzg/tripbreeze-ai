# Health and Vaccination Requirements by Destination

Use these notes as a planning guide, not a substitute for official health authority guidance. Requirements depend on the traveler's origin country, recent travel history, and trip activities. Always check official sources (CDC, WHO, national health ministries) close to departure.

- Yellow fever certificate rules depend on the countries you have recently visited, not only your destination.
- Routine vaccinations (MMR, DTP, hepatitis A/B, COVID-19) should be up to date regardless of destination.
- Travel health insurance with medical evacuation coverage is strongly recommended worldwide.

## France (Schengen Area)
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry from most low-risk countries. Routine vaccinations recommended.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A and B for longer stays or rural travel. Rabies for extended outdoor trips.
- **Health insurance:** Schengen visa applicants must show travel insurance with minimum €30,000 medical coverage. Visa-free travelers are not formally required to carry it but should.
- **Tap water:** Safe to drink throughout the country.
- **Pharmacies:** Widely available, identifiable by green cross signs. Many medications require a prescription.
- **Special note:** EU/EEA travelers can use the European Health Insurance Card (EHIC/GHIC) for public healthcare.

## Japan
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations plus hepatitis A. Japanese encephalitis for rural stays in summer.
- **Health insurance:** Not required for entry but strongly recommended — healthcare costs are high and foreign insurance is often required up front.
- **Tap water:** Safe to drink nationwide.
- **Pharmacies:** Widespread in cities. Some Western prescription drugs (including certain ADHD and pain medications) are banned — check before traveling.
- **Special note:** Bring a Yakkan Shoumei import certificate for personal prescriptions exceeding one month's supply.

## United States
- **Required vaccines:** None for most travelers. Routine vaccinations recommended.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Influenza seasonally. Hepatitis A and B for longer stays.
- **Health insurance:** No formal entry requirement, but travel medical insurance is strongly recommended — US healthcare costs are the highest in the world.
- **Tap water:** Generally safe in municipal systems. Bottled water common in areas with older plumbing.
- **Pharmacies:** Widespread. Many common-elsewhere medications require a US prescription.
- **Special note:** Emergency room visits without insurance can exceed $10,000 — travel insurance with evacuation coverage is the single most important preparation.

## Thailand
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from a country with risk of yellow fever transmission.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, Japanese encephalitis for rural stays, rabies for outdoor or animal-contact travelers.
- **Malaria:** Low risk in main tourist areas. Higher risk near the Myanmar, Laos, and Cambodia borders — consult a travel clinic for prophylaxis.
- **Dengue:** Year-round risk, highest during the rainy season (May–October). Use repellent.
- **Tap water:** Not recommended for drinking. Use bottled or filtered water.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended. Private hospitals in Bangkok and Phuket expect payment up front.

## Spain (Schengen Area)
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations. Hepatitis A and B for longer stays.
- **Health insurance:** Schengen visa applicants need €30,000 minimum medical coverage. EU/EEA travelers can use EHIC/GHIC.
- **Tap water:** Safe to drink in most cities; some travelers prefer bottled in rural areas.
- **Pharmacies:** Widely available, green cross signage.

## UAE (Dubai)
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from a country with risk of yellow fever transmission.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations plus hepatitis A and B.
- **Health insurance:** Mandatory for residence visas. Tourists are not strictly required to show insurance but expats and long-stay visitors must have local coverage. Travel insurance strongly recommended.
- **Tap water:** Desalinated and technically safe, but most residents and travelers drink bottled water for taste.
- **Special note:** Bringing certain medications (including some over-the-counter drugs containing codeine, tramadol, or CBD) can result in arrest. Carry prescriptions and check the Ministry of Health controlled substances list.

## Italy (Schengen Area)
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations plus hepatitis A for longer stays.
- **Health insurance:** Schengen visa applicants must have €30,000 minimum medical coverage. EU/EEA travelers use EHIC/GHIC.
- **Tap water:** Safe across the country; public fountains (nasoni) in Rome are drinkable.
- **Pharmacies:** Widely available. Many remain closed for lunch and on Sundays — check for 24-hour (farmacia di turno) rotations.

## Germany (Schengen Area)
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations. Tick-borne encephalitis for hikers in southern forests during spring and summer.
- **Health insurance:** Schengen visa applicants need €30,000 coverage. EU/EEA travelers use EHIC/GHIC.
- **Tap water:** Safe and high quality nationwide.

## Netherlands (Schengen Area)
- **Required vaccines:** None.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations. Hepatitis A for long stays.
- **Health insurance:** Schengen rules apply for visa holders. EU/EEA use EHIC/GHIC.
- **Tap water:** Safe and highly rated.
- **Special note:** Pharmacies (apotheek) close earlier than in many countries. Emergency pharmacies are published in local listings.

## Singapore
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from a country with risk of yellow fever transmission.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations plus hepatitis A for longer stays.
- **Dengue:** Present year-round; use repellent.
- **Tap water:** Safe to drink nationwide.
- **Health insurance:** Not required for entry but strongly recommended — private medical care is excellent but expensive.
- **Special note:** Some medications legal elsewhere (including certain sleeping pills and ADHD drugs) are controlled substances. Carry prescriptions and check the Health Sciences Authority list.

## India
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from a country with risk of yellow fever transmission.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, Japanese encephalitis for rural/monsoon travel, rabies for extended stays, cholera for high-risk areas.
- **Malaria:** Risk varies by region and season. Consult a travel clinic 4–6 weeks before departure.
- **Tap water:** Not safe for drinking. Use bottled, filtered, or boiled water. Avoid ice and raw produce washed in tap water.
- **Air quality:** Major northern cities (Delhi, Agra) can have hazardous air quality October–February. Consider N95 masks if sensitive.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended; private hospitals expect payment up front.

## Indonesia (Bali)
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from a country with risk of yellow fever transmission.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, Japanese encephalitis for longer stays, rabies for outdoor/animal-contact travelers.
- **Dengue:** Year-round risk; use repellent.
- **Malaria:** Very low risk in Bali; higher in rural Papua and parts of Sumatra.
- **Tap water:** Not safe. Bottled water only. Ice in reputable establishments is typically fine.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended — medical evacuation from outer islands can be very expensive.
- **Special note:** Monkey bites at cultural sites carry rabies risk. Seek immediate treatment if bitten.

## Vietnam
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from a country with risk of yellow fever transmission.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, Japanese encephalitis, rabies.
- **Malaria:** Low risk in cities and main tourist areas; present in remote rural areas.
- **Dengue:** Year-round, peaks in rainy season.
- **Tap water:** Not safe. Use bottled or filtered water.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended.

## Brazil
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from a country with risk of yellow fever transmission, and recommended for travelers visiting the Amazon region.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, rabies for outdoor travelers.
- **Malaria:** Risk in Amazon region. Consult a travel clinic.
- **Dengue, Zika, chikungunya:** Present year-round, especially in rainy season. Pregnant travelers should consult official Zika guidance.
- **Tap water:** Generally treated but bottled water common for travelers.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended.

## Mexico
- **Required vaccines:** None routinely.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A, typhoid, hepatitis B for longer stays.
- **Malaria:** Low risk in most tourist areas; higher in rural Chiapas and Oaxaca.
- **Dengue:** Present in coastal and tropical regions.
- **Tap water:** Not safe for drinking. Use bottled or filtered water. Ice in reputable hotels and restaurants is usually made from purified water.
- **Altitude:** Mexico City sits at 2,240m — travelers arriving from sea level may feel short of breath for a day or two.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended.

## Australia
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from a country with risk of yellow fever transmission.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations. Japanese encephalitis for some rural northern areas.
- **Tap water:** Safe nationwide.
- **UV exposure:** Extremely high UV index — high-SPF sunscreen, hats, and sun-protective clothing are essential.
- **Health insurance:** UK, NZ, and a few other countries have reciprocal Medicare agreements for emergency care. All other travelers should have travel insurance.

## Kenya
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required for travelers arriving from (or transiting through for more than 12 hours) a country with risk of yellow fever transmission.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, rabies, cholera for some areas.
- **Malaria:** Year-round risk in most of the country below 2,500m. Prophylaxis strongly recommended; Nairobi itself is low risk.
- **Tap water:** Not safe. Use bottled, filtered, or boiled water.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended with medical evacuation coverage — many safari destinations are hours from a hospital.

## Peru
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate recommended for travelers visiting Amazon regions (under 2,300m).
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, rabies for outdoor travelers.
- **Altitude:** Cusco (3,400m) and Lake Titicaca (3,800m) cause altitude sickness in many travelers. Acclimatize gradually, avoid alcohol on arrival, and consider acetazolamide after consulting a doctor.
- **Tap water:** Not safe. Bottled water only.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended, especially for trekking trips.

## Portugal (Schengen Area)
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations. Hepatitis A and B for longer stays.
- **Health insurance:** Schengen visa applicants need €30,000 minimum medical coverage. EU/EEA travelers can use EHIC/GHIC.
- **Tap water:** Safe to drink across the country, including Lisbon and Porto.
- **Pharmacies:** Widely available, green cross signage. 24-hour rotas posted at pharmacies (farmácia de serviço).

## Greece (Schengen Area)
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations. Hepatitis A for island travel.
- **Health insurance:** Schengen visa applicants need €30,000 coverage. EU/EEA travelers use EHIC/GHIC.
- **Tap water:** Safe in Athens and most of the mainland; some islands rely on desalination and many locals prefer bottled water.
- **Heat:** Summer heatwaves (July–August) routinely exceed 40°C. Carry water, avoid midday sun, and be aware of wildfire risk.
- **Pharmacies:** Widely available. Duty pharmacies rotate overnight in cities.

## Ireland
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations up to date.
- **Health insurance:** No formal entry requirement. EU/EEA travelers use EHIC/GHIC; UK travelers have reciprocal arrangements. Non-EU travelers should carry travel insurance.
- **Tap water:** Safe nationwide.
- **Special note:** Pharmacies (chemists) are widespread but operate shorter hours than in many countries. Bring a supply of any regular prescription medicine.

## Switzerland (Schengen Area)
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations. Tick-borne encephalitis recommended for hikers across most of the country between spring and autumn.
- **Health insurance:** Schengen visa applicants need €30,000 coverage. EU/EEA EHIC/GHIC is accepted at public providers but Switzerland is not in the EU — private clinics may require direct payment.
- **Tap water:** Exceptional quality; drinkable from public fountains in most cities and trailhead taps.
- **Altitude:** Jungfraujoch (3,454m) and other high-altitude day trips can cause mild altitude effects in travelers arriving from sea level.
- **Special note:** Medical costs are among the highest in Europe — robust travel insurance is strongly recommended.

## Norway (Schengen Area)
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations. Tick-borne encephalitis for forested areas along the southern coast during spring and summer.
- **Health insurance:** Schengen visa applicants need €30,000 coverage. EU/EEA EHIC/GHIC is accepted at public providers (Norway participates despite being non-EU).
- **Tap water:** Safe and highly rated nationwide.
- **Special note:** Summer mosquito activity in the north can be heavy — pack repellent for outdoor trips above the Arctic Circle.

## Czech Republic (Schengen Area)
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Routine vaccinations. Tick-borne encephalitis for hikers in forested areas during spring and summer.
- **Health insurance:** Schengen visa applicants need €30,000 coverage. EU/EEA travelers use EHIC/GHIC.
- **Tap water:** Safe to drink across the country.
- **Pharmacies:** "Lékárna" signs. Most cities have 24-hour options; over-the-counter availability is similar to Germany.

## Argentina
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required for travelers arriving from (or transiting) certain countries with risk of yellow fever transmission.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Yellow fever for visits to Iguazu Falls, northern provinces, and Misiones. Hepatitis A and B, typhoid for longer stays.
- **Altitude:** Northwest provinces (Salta, Jujuy, Purmamarca) sit above 2,500m. Acclimatize gradually.
- **Dengue:** Active transmission in northern Argentina during summer (November–April).
- **Tap water:** Generally safe in Buenos Aires and major cities; bottled water common in the north and Patagonia wilderness.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended; public hospitals will treat emergencies but private care is preferred and expects payment.

## Morocco
- **Required vaccines:** None for entry.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, rabies for longer or outdoor stays.
- **Tap water:** Not recommended for drinking. Bottled water only. Be cautious with salads and fruit washed in tap water.
- **Food safety:** Street food is a major attraction but choose busy, freshly cooked stalls. Tagines and grilled items are lower-risk than raw salads.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended — private clinics in Casablanca, Marrakech, and Rabat expect payment up front.

## Egypt
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from a country with risk of yellow fever transmission.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, rabies for extended stays, polio booster for some travelers.
- **Schistosomiasis:** Do not swim or wade in the Nile or freshwater canals — schistosomiasis is endemic.
- **Tap water:** Not recommended for drinking. Use bottled or filtered water and avoid ice from informal vendors.
- **Heat:** Summer temperatures in Luxor and Aswan regularly exceed 45°C. Limit outdoor activity to mornings and evenings.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended.

## Philippines
- **Required vaccines:** Yellow fever certificate required if arriving from a country with risk of yellow fever transmission.
- **Recommended vaccines:** Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, Japanese encephalitis for rural stays, rabies for outdoor or long-stay travelers.
- **Malaria:** Present in some rural areas of Palawan and Mindanao; low risk in Manila, Cebu, Boracay.
- **Dengue:** Year-round risk, peaks during the rainy season (June–November).
- **Tap water:** Not safe. Bottled or filtered water. Many hotels provide in-room filtered jugs.
- **Health insurance:** Strongly recommended; medical evacuation from remote islands can be expensive.
