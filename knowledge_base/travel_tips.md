# General Travel Tips and Advice

## Booking Flights
- **Book early:** Domestic flights 1–3 months ahead, international flights 2–6 months ahead for best prices.
- **Be flexible with dates:** Mid-week flights (Tuesday–Thursday) are often cheaper. Use flexible date search.
- **Consider layovers:** One-stop flights can save 20–40% compared to direct flights.
- **Budget airlines:** Check baggage fees — a "cheap" ticket with luggage fees can cost more than a full-service airline.
- **Red-eye flights:** Overnight flights save on hotel costs and maximize daytime at destination.
- **Airline alliances:** Stick to one alliance (Star Alliance, SkyTeam, Oneworld) to accumulate miles.
- **Incognito browsing:** Flight prices may increase with repeated searches — use private/incognito mode.
- **Nearby airports:** Check alternate airports near your destination — they can be significantly cheaper (e.g., Girona for Barcelona, Beauvais for Paris).

## Booking Hotels
- **Location matters:** Pay a bit more to stay central — you'll save on transport and time.
- **Read recent reviews:** Focus on reviews from the last 3–6 months for accurate info.
- **Consider alternatives:** Apartments, hostels, or guesthouses can offer better value and local experience.
- **Loyalty programs:** Hotel chain loyalty programs offer free nights, upgrades, and perks.
- **Book directly:** Hotels often offer best-price guarantees and perks for direct bookings.
- **Check cancellation policy:** Flexible cancellation is worth a small premium for peace of mind.
- **Ask for upgrades:** Politely asking at check-in for available upgrades costs nothing and sometimes works, especially during low season or for loyalty members.
- **Check-in timing:** Hotels typically check in at 2–3 PM. Arrive early? Ask to store luggage; many hotels have luggage rooms.

## Budgeting
- **Use the 50/30/20 rule for travel budgets:**
  - 50% on accommodation and transport
  - 30% on food, activities, and experiences
  - 20% as a buffer for emergencies and splurges
- **Track expenses:** Use apps like Trail Wallet or Splitwise for group trips.
- **ATM withdrawals:** Use banks that reimburse foreign ATM fees. Avoid airport exchange counters.
- **Tipping customs:** Research local tipping norms — varies dramatically by country.
  - **USA/Canada:** 18–20% at restaurants, $1–2 per drink at bars
  - **Europe:** Rounding up or 5–10% is common; service is often included
  - **Japan:** No tipping — it can be considered rude
  - **Southeast Asia:** Not expected but appreciated (10% at upscale restaurants)
  - **Middle East:** 10–15% if service charge not included
- **Travel insurance:** Always get it. Medical emergencies abroad can be financially devastating. A single hospital visit can cost $10,000+ without insurance.
- **Multi-currency cards:** Get a card with no foreign transaction fees (Wise, Revolut, Charles Schwab). Avoid dynamic currency conversion at point-of-sale — always pay in local currency.
- **Daily budget tracking:** Set a daily spend limit and check each evening. It's easier to adjust early than discover you're over budget at the end.

## Packing Tips
- **Carry-on only:** Saves baggage fees and time at airports. Pack versatile clothing.
- **Essential documents:** Passport, visa docs, travel insurance, hotel confirmations — keep digital copies in cloud storage.
- **Universal adapter:** One adapter covers most countries. Check voltage compatibility for electronics.
- **Medications:** Carry prescriptions in original packaging with doctor's note. Pack in carry-on, not checked luggage.
- **Layers over bulk:** Three light layers are more versatile than one heavy jacket. Works for both warm restaurants and cold evenings.
- **Packing cubes:** Organize clothes by day or type. Compression cubes save space. Roll clothes to reduce wrinkles.
- **Laundry strategy:** Pack for 5–7 days regardless of trip length. Most destinations have laundromats or hotel laundry services.

## Safety
- **Register with embassy:** Many countries offer traveler registration for emergencies.
- **Share itinerary:** Give a trusted contact your travel plans and check-in schedule.
- **Secure valuables:** Use hotel safes. Carry a money belt for high-risk areas.
- **Local emergency numbers:** Save them in your phone before arrival.
- **Travel insurance:** Covers medical, theft, cancellation — essential for international travel.
- **Copies of documents:** Email yourself scans of your passport, visa, insurance, and credit cards. Store in cloud storage accessible from any device.
- **Situational awareness:** Keep your phone in a front pocket. Wear backpacks in front on crowded public transit. Don't count cash in public.
- **Hotel safety:** Use the door chain/latch. Don't open the door for unexpected visitors. Check fire escape routes on arrival.

## Health
- **Vaccinations:** Check CDC or WHO recommendations 6–8 weeks before travel.
- **Water safety:** In many countries, drink only bottled or purified water. This includes ice cubes and brushing teeth.
- **Jet lag:** Adjust sleep schedule before departure. Stay hydrated. Get sunlight at destination. Melatonin can help reset your clock.
- **Altitude sickness:** Acclimatize gradually at high-altitude destinations (Cusco, La Paz, Lhasa, Kathmandu). Symptoms include headache, nausea, dizziness. Descend if symptoms worsen.
- **Sun protection:** Pack reef-safe sunscreen (SPF 50+), sunglasses, and a hat. Reapply every 2 hours, especially near water. UV is strongest 10 AM–4 PM.
- **Motion sickness:** Sit in the front of cars, mid-section of boats, over the wing on planes. Ginger or medication (dramamine) can help. Look at the horizon.
- **Food safety abroad:** Eat where locals eat (high turnover = fresh food). "Cook it, peel it, or leave it" is the classic rule for developing countries. Street food that's cooked fresh in front of you is usually safer than buffets.
- **Travel pharmacy essentials:** Pain relief, anti-diarrheal, rehydration salts, antihistamine, band-aids, hand sanitizer, insect repellent (DEET-based for tropical destinations).

## Cultural Etiquette
- **Dress codes:** Research dress expectations before visiting religious sites. Many require covered shoulders and knees (mosques, temples, churches). Carry a scarf for quick cover-ups.
- **Photography:** Always ask before photographing people, especially in developing countries and indigenous communities. Some religious sites prohibit photography entirely.
- **Greetings:** Learn the local greeting — even a basic "hello" and "thank you" in the local language is always appreciated and often changes how you're treated.
- **Shoes off:** In Japan, Thailand, parts of India, and many Asian countries, remove shoes before entering homes, temples, and some restaurants. Wear easy slip-on/off shoes.
- **Left hand:** In many Middle Eastern, African, and South Asian cultures, the left hand is considered unclean. Use your right hand for eating, handshakes, and passing items.
- **Haggling:** Expected in many markets worldwide (Morocco, Turkey, Southeast Asia, India). Not appropriate in fixed-price shops. Start at 30–50% of asking price and work up. Walk away if the price isn't right — they'll often call you back.
- **Dining customs:** In many European countries, you must ask for the bill — it won't be brought automatically. In Japan, slurping noodles is polite. In India, eating with your hands is common and acceptable.
- **Religious sensitivity:** During Ramadan in Muslim countries, avoid eating/drinking in public during daylight hours. In Buddhist temples, never point feet at Buddha statues. In Hindu temples, walk clockwise.
