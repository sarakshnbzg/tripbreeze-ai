# Popular Destination Guides

## Paris, France
- **Best time to visit:** April–June and September–October for mild weather and fewer crowds. July–August is peak tourist season with long queues. December is magical for Christmas markets but cold and grey.
- **Top attractions:** Eiffel Tower, Louvre Museum, Notre-Dame Cathedral, Montmartre, Champs-Élysées, Musée d'Orsay, Sacré-Cœur, Palace of Versailles (day trip).
- **Neighborhoods:** Le Marais (trendy boutiques, LGBTQ+ friendly), Saint-Germain-des-Prés (classic cafés, bookshops), Montmartre (artistic, hilly, Sacré-Cœur views), Bastille (nightlife, young crowd), Latin Quarter (student area, budget-friendly dining).
- **Local transport:** Paris Métro is efficient and affordable. Buy a carnet of 10 tickets or a Navigo weekly pass. Vélib' bike-share is great for sightseeing.
- **Food recommendations:** Croissants from a local boulangerie, crêpes in Montparnasse, steak-frites at a classic bistro, falafel in Le Marais (L'As du Fallafel), wine and cheese at a cave à manger. Avoid restaurants directly facing major tourist sites — walk one block away for better value.
- **Budget tip:** Many museums are free on the first Sunday of each month. Picnicking along the Seine saves on dining costs. The Paris Museum Pass covers 50+ attractions.
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR). Credit cards widely accepted; some small shops have a €5–€10 minimum.
- **Language:** French. English is spoken in tourist areas but attempting French is appreciated.
- **Safety:** Generally safe. Watch for pickpockets at tourist hotspots and on the Métro. Common scams: petition signers at Sacré-Cœur, bracelet sellers at Trocadéro, shell game hustlers near tourist areas.
- **Average daily budget:** €100–€200 mid-range; €50–€80 budget.

## Tokyo, Japan
- **Best time to visit:** March–May (cherry blossom season peaks late March to mid-April) and September–November (autumn foliage). Summer (June–August) is hot and humid with a rainy season in June. Winter is mild and dry with fewer tourists.
- **Top attractions:** Shibuya Crossing, Senso-ji Temple, Meiji Shrine, Tsukiji Outer Market, Akihabara, Tokyo Skytree, Shinjuku Gyoen, TeamLab exhibits.
- **Neighborhoods:** Shinjuku (neon-lit nightlife, Golden Gai's tiny bars), Shibuya (youth culture, fashion), Harajuku (cosplay, Takeshita Street), Asakusa (traditional temples, old Tokyo feel), Shimokitazawa (vintage shops, indie cafés), Roppongi (expat nightlife, art museums).
- **Local transport:** JR Pass for tourists covers most trains. Suica/Pasmo IC cards for subway and buses. Trains stop around midnight — plan accordingly or budget for taxis.
- **Food recommendations:** Ramen in Shinjuku (try Fuunji for tsukemen), sushi at Tsukiji Outer Market, tempura at a counter-style shop, yakitori under the Yurakucho tracks, conveyor belt sushi (Genki Sushi), izakaya pub-style dining in Shinbashi. Convenience store (konbini) food is surprisingly excellent.
- **Budget tip:** Conveyor belt sushi and ramen shops offer excellent meals for ¥500–¥1,000. 100-yen shops for souvenirs. Lunch sets (teishoku) are 30–50% cheaper than dinner at the same restaurant.
- **Currency:** Japanese Yen (JPY). Cash is still king in many places; carry yen. 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards.
- **Language:** Japanese. English signage in major stations; limited English elsewhere. Download Google Translate offline pack.
- **Safety:** One of the safest major cities in the world. Very low crime rate. Biggest risk is getting lost in the subway system.
- **Average daily budget:** ¥10,000–¥20,000 mid-range; ¥5,000–¥8,000 budget.

## New York City, USA
- **Best time to visit:** April–June and September–November for pleasant weather. December for holiday lights (but cold and crowded). Summer is hot and humid. January–February is cheapest but bitterly cold.
- **Top attractions:** Statue of Liberty, Central Park, Times Square, Brooklyn Bridge, Metropolitan Museum of Art, Empire State Building, High Line, 9/11 Memorial.
- **Neighborhoods:** Manhattan Midtown (Times Square, theaters, tourist hub), SoHo (shopping, galleries), Williamsburg Brooklyn (hipster, craft breweries, vintage), Greenwich Village (jazz clubs, Washington Square Park), Harlem (gospel brunch, soul food), Lower East Side (diverse food scene, nightlife), Upper West Side (quieter, near Central Park and museums).
- **Local transport:** NYC Subway runs 24/7. Get an OMNY contactless pass or MetroCard. Avoid taxis in rush hour — subway is faster. Citi Bike for short trips.
- **Food recommendations:** Dollar pizza slices, bagels with lox (Russ & Daughters), pastrami sandwich at Katz's Deli, dim sum in Chinatown, smoked meats in Brooklyn, food trucks throughout Midtown. Halal cart on 53rd and 6th is iconic.
- **Budget tip:** Free Staten Island Ferry for Statue of Liberty views. Many museums have pay-what-you-wish hours. Central Park, High Line, and Brooklyn Bridge are free.
- **Currency:** US Dollar (USD). Cards accepted almost everywhere; tips expected (18–20%).
- **Language:** English. Highly multilingual city.
- **Safety:** Generally safe in tourist areas. Standard big-city precautions apply. Avoid empty subway cars late at night.
- **Average daily budget:** $150–$300 mid-range; $80–$120 budget.

## Bangkok, Thailand
- **Best time to visit:** November–February (cool and dry season, 25–32°C). March–May is scorching hot (35–40°C). June–October is rainy but less crowded; rain is usually short afternoon bursts.
- **Top attractions:** Grand Palace, Wat Pho, Chatuchak Weekend Market, Khao San Road, Chao Phraya River, Wat Arun, Jim Thompson House.
- **Neighborhoods:** Sukhumvit (expat hub, rooftop bars, malls), Silom/Sathorn (business district, night markets), Khao San Road (backpacker central), Chinatown/Yaowarat (street food heaven, especially at night), Thonburi (quieter west bank, Wat Arun), Ari (hipster cafés, local feel).
- **Local transport:** BTS Skytrain and MRT subway. Tuk-tuks and river boats for short trips. Grab app for ride-hailing (more reliable than street taxis).
- **Food recommendations:** Pad Thai from a street vendor, som tum (papaya salad), mango sticky rice, boat noodles at Victory Monument, tom yum soup, khao man gai (chicken rice). Yaowarat (Chinatown) at night is a street food paradise.
- **Budget tip:** Street food is excellent and cheap (฿30–฿60 per dish). Negotiate prices at markets. Temples often have small entry fees (฿50–฿200). River ferry is one of the cheapest ways to sightsee.
- **Currency:** Thai Baht (THB). ATMs widely available; credit cards accepted at malls. ATMs charge ฿220 foreign transaction fee.
- **Language:** Thai. English spoken in tourist areas and hotels.
- **Safety:** Generally safe. Be cautious of common tourist scams: gem shop scams ("today is a special government sale"), inflated tuk-tuk prices (agree on fare beforehand), jet ski damage scams on islands. Avoid buying drinks from strangers at nightclubs.
- **Average daily budget:** ฿1,500–฿3,000 mid-range; ฿800–฿1,200 budget.

## Barcelona, Spain
- **Best time to visit:** May–June and September–October for warm weather without extreme heat. July–August peaks at 33°C+ with massive crowds. Winter is mild (10–15°C) and much quieter.
- **Top attractions:** Sagrada Familia, Park Güell, La Rambla, Gothic Quarter, Camp Nou, Casa Batlló, La Boqueria Market, Barceloneta Beach.
- **Neighborhoods:** Gothic Quarter (medieval streets, bars, restaurants), El Born (boutiques, Picasso Museum), Gràcia (village feel, local bars, Park Güell), Eixample (modernist architecture, upscale dining), Barceloneta (beach, seafood), Raval (multicultural, edgy, street art).
- **Local transport:** TMB Metro is efficient. T-Casual card for 10 trips. Walking is the best way to explore the old town.
- **Food recommendations:** Patatas bravas, pan con tomate, jamón ibérico, paella (better at beachside restaurants), pintxos/tapas in El Born, churros con chocolate at a xurreria. Eat dinner late — locals dine at 9–10 PM.
- **Budget tip:** Free walking tours available. Beach access is free. Lunch menú del día offers 3-course meals for €10–€15. Book Sagrada Familia tickets online well in advance — sells out.
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR).
- **Language:** Catalan and Spanish. English widely understood in tourist areas.
- **Safety:** Watch for pickpockets on La Rambla, in the Metro, and at the beach. Common scams: fake charity clipboard signers, someone "finding" a ring and offering it to you. Don't leave belongings unattended on the beach.
- **Average daily budget:** €80–€150 mid-range; €40–€70 budget.

## Madrid, Spain
- **Best time to visit:** April–June and September–October for warm weather, long evenings, and manageable crowds. July–August can be very hot (often 35°C+) and many locals leave the city. Winter is cool, dry, and quieter.
- **Top attractions:** Prado Museum, Reina Sofia, Royal Palace, Retiro Park, Plaza Mayor, Puerta del Sol, Gran Via, Temple of Debod.
- **Neighborhoods:** Sol/Gran Via (central, busy, best for first-time visitors), Malasana (trendy cafes, nightlife, vintage shops), Chueca (LGBTQ+ friendly, lively dining and bars), La Latina (tapas, old streets, Sunday market), Salamanca (upscale shopping, elegant boulevards), Lavapies (multicultural, artsy, budget-friendly eats).
- **Local transport:** Madrid Metro is extensive, clean, and easy to use. Multi-day transport passes offer good value. The center is very walkable, and Cercanias trains help with airport access and day trips.
- **Food recommendations:** Bocadillo de calamares near Plaza Mayor, tortilla espanola, croquetas, patatas bravas, churros con chocolate at San Gines, vermouth on tap in La Latina, and late-night tapas bar hopping.
- **Budget tip:** Many major museums have free-entry evening hours on selected days. Menu del dia lunches offer strong value. Retiro Park, Temple of Debod sunset views, and wandering historic neighborhoods are free.
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR).
- **Language:** Spanish. English is common in central hotels, restaurants, and tourist areas.
- **Safety:** Generally safe. Watch for pickpockets on the Metro, around Sol, Plaza Mayor, and busy nightlife areas. Use standard big-city precautions late at night.
- **Average daily budget:** EUR 90–160 mid-range; EUR 45–80 budget.

## Dubai, UAE
- **Best time to visit:** November–March for cooler temperatures (20–30°C). April–October is brutally hot (40–50°C) — outdoor activities are impossible. Ramadan dates shift yearly; restaurants close during daytime fasting hours but hotels accommodate tourists.
- **Top attractions:** Burj Khalifa, Dubai Mall, Palm Jumeirah, Dubai Marina, Gold Souk, Dubai Frame, Desert Safari, Global Village (seasonal).
- **Neighborhoods:** Downtown (Burj Khalifa, Dubai Mall), Dubai Marina (waterfront dining, walk), JBR (beach, family-friendly), Deira (old Dubai, souks, creek), Al Fahidi (historic, cultural), DIFC (fine dining, galleries).
- **Local transport:** Dubai Metro is modern and affordable. Nol card for all public transport. Taxis are cheap. No walkability between districts — always use transport.
- **Food recommendations:** Shawarma at Al Mallah, Arabic mezze, camel burger at Local House Restaurant, Iranian food in Deira, brunch culture is huge (Friday brunches at hotels are an institution). Indian food is excellent and cheap in Deira/Bur Dubai.
- **Budget tip:** Visit during shoulder season for lower hotel rates. Free beaches at JBR. Dubai Mall and fountain show are free. Deira souks offer better prices than mall shops.
- **Currency:** UAE Dirham (AED). Credit cards widely accepted.
- **Language:** Arabic. English is widely spoken as a business language.
- **Safety:** Extremely safe. Very low crime rate. Strict laws — dress modestly in public areas, no public displays of affection, zero tolerance for drugs. Respect local customs and laws during Ramadan.
- **Average daily budget:** AED 500–1,000 mid-range; AED 250–400 budget.

## Rome, Italy
- **Best time to visit:** April–May and September–October. Summer (July–August) is sweltering (35°C+) and packed. Winter is mild and quiet with shorter museum queues.
- **Top attractions:** Colosseum, Vatican Museums, Pantheon, Trevi Fountain, Roman Forum, St. Peter's Basilica, Borghese Gallery, Piazza Navona.
- **Neighborhoods:** Trastevere (cobblestoned, best evening atmosphere, trattorias), Centro Storico (Pantheon, Navona, most walkable), Monti (bohemian, vintage shops), Testaccio (working-class, serious food scene), Prati (near Vatican, quieter, good restaurants), San Lorenzo (student area, cheap eats).
- **Local transport:** Metro lines A and B. Bus network covers the rest. Roma Pass for transport + museum entry. The city center is very walkable.
- **Food recommendations:** Cacio e pepe, carbonara (no cream!), supplì (fried rice balls), pizza al taglio (by-the-slice), gelato from artisan shops (avoid places with piled-high bright colors — real gelato is flat in the pan). Testaccio is the food neighborhood. Never order cappuccino after 11 AM — Italians consider it a breakfast drink.
- **Budget tip:** Free entry to Pantheon and many churches. Drink from public water fountains (nasoni). Book Vatican and Colosseum tickets online to skip 2-hour queues.
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR).
- **Language:** Italian. English in tourist areas.
- **Safety:** Pickpocketing common near tourist sites and on buses (especially line 64 to Vatican). Common scams: gladiators at Colosseum demanding payment for photos, "free" roses placed in your hand then charged.
- **Average daily budget:** €80–€160 mid-range; €40–€70 budget.

## Sydney, Australia
- **Best time to visit:** September–November (spring) and March–May (autumn) for mild weather. Summer (December–February) is peak season with NYE fireworks. Winter (June–August) is mild (10–18°C) and quiet.
- **Top attractions:** Sydney Opera House, Harbour Bridge, Bondi Beach, Taronga Zoo, The Rocks, Royal Botanic Garden, Manly Beach, Blue Mountains (day trip).
- **Neighborhoods:** The Rocks (historic, harbor-side pubs), Surry Hills (foodie hub, cafés), Newtown (eclectic, live music, vintage), Darlinghurst (diverse dining, nightlife), Manly (beach suburb, ferry ride), Bondi (beach culture, coastal walks).
- **Local transport:** Opal card for trains, buses, ferries, and light rail. Ferry to Manly is one of the world's best commutes.
- **Food recommendations:** Flat white coffee (invented here), avocado toast (an Aussie institution), fish and chips at Bondi, meat pies, Asian food in Chinatown or Haymarket, brunch in Surry Hills (Bills is famous). Sydney has world-class pan-Asian cuisine.
- **Budget tip:** Many coastal walks are free (Bondi to Coogee). BYO restaurants save on dining. Sunday session at a pub is a local tradition.
- **Currency:** Australian Dollar (AUD). Almost entirely cashless — card everywhere.
- **Language:** English.
- **Safety:** Very safe. Sun protection is essential — UV index is extremely high. Swim between the flags at beaches. Jellyfish and rips can be dangerous.
- **Average daily budget:** AUD 150–300 mid-range; AUD 80–120 budget.

## Istanbul, Turkey
- **Best time to visit:** April–May and September–November. Summer (July–August) is hot (30–35°C) and humid. Winter is cold and rainy but uncrowded. Tulip Festival in April is stunning.
- **Top attractions:** Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque, Grand Bazaar, Topkapi Palace, Bosphorus Cruise, Basilica Cistern, Spice Bazaar, Galata Tower.
- **Neighborhoods:** Sultanahmet (historic core, major mosques and palaces), Beyoğlu/Galata (hip cafés, Istiklal Avenue nightlife), Kadıköy (Asian side, local food market, artsy), Balat (colorful streets, Instagram-famous), Beşiktaş (local nightlife, waterfront), Karaköy (street art, brunch spots).
- **Local transport:** Istanbulkart for metro, tram, bus, and ferries. Ferries across the Bosphorus are scenic and cheap. Traffic is notorious — avoid taxis during rush hour.
- **Food recommendations:** Kebabs (beyond döner — try Adana and Iskender), balık ekmek (fish sandwich at Eminönü), simit (sesame bread ring), manti (Turkish dumplings), Turkish breakfast spread (weekend brunch is a cultural event), baklava from Karaköy Güllüoğlu.
- **Budget tip:** Street food (simit, balık ekmek) is delicious and cheap. Haggle at the Grand Bazaar (start at 50% of asking price). Museum Pass Istanbul covers major sites. Asian side restaurants are significantly cheaper than European side.
- **Currency:** Turkish Lira (TRY). USD and EUR sometimes accepted at tourist shops. Carry lira for best prices.
- **Language:** Turkish. English in tourist areas.
- **Safety:** Generally safe for tourists. Standard precautions in crowded areas. Scams: taxi drivers taking long routes (use BiTaksi app instead), shoe-shine trick (someone drops a brush, you pick it up, they insist on shining your shoes then overcharge).
- **Average daily budget:** ₺800–₺1,500 mid-range; ₺400–₺700 budget.

## Bali, Indonesia
- **Best time to visit:** April–October (dry season). Peak season is July–August and Christmas/New Year. November–March is rainy season — afternoon showers but greener landscapes and fewer tourists.
- **Top attractions:** Ubud Rice Terraces (Tegallalang), Tanah Lot Temple, Uluwatu Temple (kecak fire dance at sunset), Seminyak Beach, Mount Batur (sunrise trek), Sacred Monkey Forest, Tirta Empul water temple.
- **Neighborhoods:** Ubud (cultural heart, rice terraces, yoga), Seminyak (beach clubs, upscale dining), Canggu (surfer/digital nomad hub, hipster cafés), Uluwatu (cliff-top temples, luxury resorts), Nusa Dua (resort area, calm waters), Sanur (quiet, sunrise beach, family-friendly).
- **Local transport:** Scooter rental is popular but risky (chaotic traffic, international license technically required). Grab (ride-hailing) is convenient. Hire a private driver for day trips (Rp 500,000–700,000/day).
- **Food recommendations:** Nasi goreng (fried rice), babi guling (suckling pig in Ubud — try Ibu Oka), sate lilit (Balinese satay), lawar (mixed vegetables with coconut), smoothie bowls in Canggu, fresh seafood at Jimbaran Bay beach BBQ. Warungs (local eateries) are the best value.
- **Budget tip:** Warungs serve meals for Rp 20,000–40,000. Negotiate for tours and transport. Temples charge Rp 30,000–50,000 entry. Renting a scooter is cheapest at Rp 70,000/day. Avoid Kuta for better value and experience.
- **Currency:** Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). ATMs widely available. Be wary of card skimming at standalone ATMs — use bank-attached machines.
- **Language:** Indonesian (Bahasa). English widely spoken in tourist areas.
- **Safety:** Generally safe. Watch for traffic (especially on scooter), strong ocean currents, and monkeys at Monkey Forest (secure belongings). Common scam: money changers in Kuta offering great rates then miscounting bills.
- **Average daily budget:** Rp 500,000–1,500,000 mid-range; Rp 250,000–500,000 budget.

## London, United Kingdom
- **Best time to visit:** May–September for longest days and warmest weather (18–25°C). June–August is peak season. Spring (April–May) has blooming parks. Winter is grey and cold but festive in December.
- **Top attractions:** British Museum, Tower of London, Buckingham Palace, Westminster Abbey, London Eye, Borough Market, Hyde Park, West End theatre.
- **Neighborhoods:** Soho (nightlife, restaurants, theatre district), Shoreditch (street art, vintage, hipster bars), South Bank (Tate Modern, Thames walks), Camden (market, punk heritage, live music), Notting Hill (pastel houses, Portobello Road market), Covent Garden (street performers, shopping), Brixton (Caribbean and African food scene).
- **Local transport:** Oyster card or contactless payment for Tube, buses, Overground. Daily cap applies. Tube is fastest but walking across central London is often quicker for short distances.
- **Food recommendations:** Full English breakfast, Sunday roast at a pub, fish and chips (try Poppies in Spitalfields), Borough Market for artisan food stalls, curry on Brick Lane, afternoon tea (Sketch or Fortnum & Mason for a splurge). London's food scene is now world-class across every cuisine.
- **Budget tip:** Many major museums are free (British Museum, Tate Modern, Natural History Museum, V&A). TKTS booth in Leicester Square for discounted theatre tickets. Pub meals are decent value (£10–£15).
- **Currency:** British Pound (GBP). Contactless payment is ubiquitous.
- **Language:** English.
- **Safety:** Very safe. Watch for pickpockets on the Tube and at markets. Scams: fake charity collectors, three-card monte on Westminster Bridge.
- **Average daily budget:** £100–£250 mid-range; £50–£90 budget.

## Amsterdam, Netherlands
- **Best time to visit:** April–May (tulip season, Keukenhof gardens) and September. Summer (June–August) is warm and busy. King's Day (April 27) is a massive citywide party. Winter is cold but cozy with holiday markets.
- **Top attractions:** Anne Frank House, Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh Museum, canal ring, Vondelpark, Jordaan district, A'DAM Lookout, Heineken Experience.
- **Neighborhoods:** Jordaan (picturesque canals, indie shops, brown cafés), De Pijp (Albert Cuyp Market, diverse food, young crowd), Oud-West (Foodhallen, local feel), Centrum (tourist core, Red Light District), Noord (NDSM Wharf, creative industrial area), Plantage (botanical gardens, quiet).
- **Local transport:** Cycling is king — rent a bike (€10–€15/day) and ride like a local. GVB day passes for tram/bus/metro. Walking covers most of central Amsterdam easily.
- **Food recommendations:** Stroopwafels (fresh from market stalls), bitterballen (fried meat ragout balls at brown cafés), raw herring from a street cart, Indonesian rijsttafel (rice table — colonial-era feast), pancakes (pannenkoeken), apple pie at Winkel 43.
- **Budget tip:** Museumkaart (€65) covers 400+ museums for a year. Canal views are free — just walk. Markets (Albert Cuyp, Waterlooplein) offer cheap eats. Buy an I amsterdam City Card for transport + museums.
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR). Almost entirely cashless — card everywhere.
- **Language:** Dutch. Almost everyone speaks excellent English.
- **Safety:** Very safe. Biggest risk is bicycles — stay out of bike lanes as a pedestrian. Be aware in the Red Light District late at night. Watch for pickpockets at Centraal Station.
- **Average daily budget:** €100–€200 mid-range; €50–€80 budget.

## Berlin, Germany
- **Best time to visit:** May–September for warm weather and outdoor festivals. June is best. Winter (November–March) is cold and dark but great for Christmas markets and indoor culture.
- **Top attractions:** Brandenburg Gate, Berlin Wall Memorial, Museum Island, Reichstag, East Side Gallery, Checkpoint Charlie, Tiergarten, Berghain (nightclub).
- **Neighborhoods:** Kreuzberg (multicultural, street food, nightlife), Mitte (central, museums, historic sites), Prenzlauer Berg (family-friendly, brunch culture, Sunday flea markets), Friedrichshain (clubs, East Side Gallery, young), Neukölln (up-and-coming, diverse food, bars), Charlottenburg (West Berlin elegance, KaDeWe department store).
- **Local transport:** BVG day pass for U-Bahn, S-Bahn, tram, and bus. Berlin is very bikeable and flat.
- **Food recommendations:** Currywurst (try Curry 36), döner kebab (Berlin's unofficial dish — Mustafa's Gemüse Kebap is legendary), Schnitzel, Turkish food in Kreuzberg, Vietnamese pho in Mitte, Sunday brunch buffets, craft beer scene throughout.
- **Budget tip:** Berlin is one of Europe's cheapest capitals. Many clubs and bars have low cover charges. Free walking tours of the Berlin Wall. East Side Gallery is free. Street food is excellent value (€3–€6).
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR). More cash-dependent than other European cities — many restaurants and bars are cash-only. Carry cash.
- **Language:** German. English very widely spoken, especially by younger people.
- **Safety:** Very safe overall. Watch belongings on the U-Bahn and at tourist sites. Scams: fake ticket inspectors, overcharging at tourist-trap restaurants on Alexanderplatz.
- **Average daily budget:** €70–€150 mid-range; €35–€60 budget.

## Prague, Czech Republic
- **Best time to visit:** April–June and September–October. Summer (July–August) is warm and very crowded. December for Christmas markets. Winter is cold but atmospheric.
- **Top attractions:** Prague Castle, Charles Bridge, Old Town Square, Astronomical Clock, St. Vitus Cathedral, Petřín Hill, Jewish Quarter, Vyšehrad.
- **Neighborhoods:** Staré Město/Old Town (historic core, tourist hub), Malá Strana (below the castle, baroque architecture, quiet gardens), Vinohrady (local favourite, great restaurants, parks), Žižkov (alternative bars, TV Tower), Karlín (up-and-coming foodie area), Letná (beer garden with panoramic views).
- **Local transport:** Metro, trams, and buses with Lítačka card. Trams are scenic. Walking covers the compact old town easily.
- **Food recommendations:** Trdelník (chimney cake — touristy but fun), svíčková (marinated beef with cream sauce), vepřo-knedlo-zelo (pork, dumplings, sauerkraut), draft Pilsner Urquell at a local pub, craft beer scene in Vinohrady and Karlín. Beer is often cheaper than water.
- **Budget tip:** Beer is incredibly cheap (30–50 CZK / €1.20–€2.00 for a half-litre). Eat at local hospodas (pubs) rather than Old Town Square restaurants. Cross the river to Malá Strana for better value.
- **Currency:** Czech Koruna (CZK). Some places accept EUR but at bad rates — always pay in CZK. Cards increasingly accepted.
- **Language:** Czech. English spoken in tourist areas and by younger people.
- **Safety:** Very safe. Pickpockets on Charles Bridge and in Old Town Square are the main concern. Scam: taxi drivers overcharging — use Bolt or Liftago apps instead. Check restaurant bills for added charges.
- **Average daily budget:** CZK 2,000–4,000 mid-range; CZK 1,000–1,800 budget.

## Vienna, Austria
- **Best time to visit:** April–June and September–October. December–January for Christmas markets, New Year's concerts, and ball season. Summer (July–August) can be hot (30°C+).
- **Top attractions:** Schönbrunn Palace, St. Stephen's Cathedral, Hofburg Palace, Belvedere Museum (Klimt's The Kiss), Vienna State Opera, Naschmarkt, MuseumsQuartier.
- **Neighborhoods:** Innere Stadt/1st District (historic core, Ring Boulevard), Neubau/7th District (hipster shops, design studios), Leopoldstadt/2nd District (Prater park, up-and-coming restaurants), Mariahilf/6th District (Naschmarkt, shopping on Mariahilfer Straße), Josefstadt/8th District (charming, local, quiet).
- **Local transport:** Wiener Linien for U-Bahn, tram, and bus. 24/48/72-hour tickets available. The Ring tram (#1 or #2) is a cheap sightseeing tour.
- **Food recommendations:** Wiener Schnitzel (try Figlmüller), Sachertorte at Hotel Sacher or Demel, Apfelstrudel, Tafelspitz (boiled beef), coffee house culture (Café Central, Café Sperl). Naschmarkt has Middle Eastern, Asian, and Austrian food stalls.
- **Budget tip:** Many museums are free on the first Sunday of the month. Standing-room tickets at the Vienna Opera are just €4–€15. Parks and palaces' gardens are free.
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR). Cards widely accepted.
- **Language:** German (Austrian dialect). English widely spoken.
- **Safety:** One of the safest cities in Europe. Very low crime. Standard caution at tourist sites.
- **Average daily budget:** €90–€180 mid-range; €45–€75 budget.

## Lisbon, Portugal
- **Best time to visit:** March–May and September–October. Summer (June–August) is warm and sunny but crowded. June has Festas de Lisboa (Santos Populares — street parties, sardines, and music). Winter is mild and rainy.
- **Top attractions:** Belém Tower, Jerónimos Monastery, Alfama district, Tram 28, Time Out Market, São Jorge Castle, LX Factory, Sintra (day trip).
- **Neighborhoods:** Alfama (oldest district, fado music, narrow alleys), Bairro Alto (nightlife, bars open after midnight), Baixa (downtown, elegant plazas), Príncipe Real (trendy, LGBTQ+ friendly, garden kiosks), LX Factory (creative hub, riverside), Belém (monuments, pastéis de nata).
- **Local transport:** Viva Viagem card for metro, buses, trams, and ferries. Tram 28 is iconic but often packed — take it early morning. Hilly city — comfortable shoes essential.
- **Food recommendations:** Pastéis de nata (custard tarts — Pastéis de Belém for the original), bacalhau (salt cod, prepared 1,000 ways), bifana (pork sandwich), grilled sardines (especially in June), ginjinha (cherry liqueur shots at A Ginjinha bar). Time Out Market for all-in-one food hall experience.
- **Budget tip:** One of Western Europe's most affordable capitals. Wine is €2–€4/glass at local tascas. Miradouros (viewpoints) are free and stunning. Sintra day trip by train is cheap (€4.50 return).
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR).
- **Language:** Portuguese. English widely spoken, especially by younger people.
- **Safety:** Generally safe. Pickpocketing on Tram 28 and in Alfama is common. Don't leave valuables visible in parked cars. Avoid buying drugs in Bairro Alto — possession is decriminalized but dealing is not.
- **Average daily budget:** €60–€130 mid-range; €30–€60 budget.

## Athens, Greece
- **Best time to visit:** April–June and September–October for warm weather without extreme heat. July–August peaks at 35–40°C. Winter is mild (10–15°C) and very quiet. Easter in Greece is a spectacular cultural experience.
- **Top attractions:** Acropolis and Parthenon, Acropolis Museum, Ancient Agora, Plaka district, Temple of Olympian Zeus, National Archaeological Museum, Syntagma Square (changing of the guard).
- **Neighborhoods:** Plaka (charming, touristy, tavernas below the Acropolis), Monastiraki (flea market, street food), Psyrri (nightlife, street art, meze restaurants), Exarchia (anarchist/alternative culture, cheap eats), Koukaki (quiet, great Acropolis views, local restaurants), Kolonaki (upscale, boutiques).
- **Local transport:** Metro (3 lines), buses, and trams. Athens transport card for unlimited rides. Walking covers the historic center. Use the metro to reach Piraeus port for island ferries.
- **Food recommendations:** Souvlaki and gyros (try Kostas in Syntagma), moussaka, grilled octopus, horiatiki (Greek salad), spanakopita (spinach pie), loukoumades (honey-drizzled doughnuts). Eat at tavernas with Greek-only menus for the most authentic experience.
- **Budget tip:** Acropolis combined ticket (€30) covers 7 archaeological sites for 5 days. Street food souvlaki is €2.50–€3.50. Rooftop bars with Acropolis views — buy one drink for the view. Beach access is free at public beaches along the coast.
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR). Cash preferred at smaller tavernas and kiosks.
- **Language:** Greek. English widely spoken in tourist areas.
- **Safety:** Safe for tourists. Watch for pickpockets on crowded metro and around Omonia Square at night. Stray dogs are common but usually friendly. Avoid Exarchia at night if unfamiliar with the area.
- **Average daily budget:** €60–€120 mid-range; €30–€55 budget.

## Edinburgh, United Kingdom
- **Best time to visit:** May–September for warmest and driest weather. August for the Edinburgh Fringe Festival (world's largest arts festival — book accommodation months ahead). December–January for Hogmanay (New Year) celebrations.
- **Top attractions:** Edinburgh Castle, Royal Mile, Arthur's Seat, Holyrood Palace, Calton Hill, Scott Monument, National Museum of Scotland, Grassmarket.
- **Neighborhoods:** Old Town (Royal Mile, medieval architecture, tourist hub), New Town (Georgian elegance, Princes Street shopping), Stockbridge (village feel, indie shops, Sunday market), Leith (waterfront, restaurants, hipster pubs), Grassmarket (pubs, street performers, castle views).
- **Local transport:** Lothian Buses with day passes. City is compact and walkable but very hilly. Tram connects airport to city center.
- **Food recommendations:** Haggis, neeps and tatties (try it — better than it sounds), Scotch pie, fish and chips, Scottish smoked salmon, cranachan (whisky cream dessert). Whisky tasting at the Scotch Whisky Experience. Pub grub is hearty and affordable.
- **Budget tip:** National Museum of Scotland is free. Climbing Arthur's Seat is free with incredible views. Free Fringe shows in August. Student pubs offer cheap meals and drinks.
- **Currency:** British Pound (GBP). Scottish banknotes are valid everywhere in the UK.
- **Language:** English (with a Scottish accent).
- **Safety:** Very safe. Standard precautions on busy streets during festival season.
- **Average daily budget:** £80–£180 mid-range; £40–£70 budget.

## Copenhagen, Denmark
- **Best time to visit:** May–August for long days (17+ hours of light in June) and outdoor life. December for Tivoli Christmas market. Winter is cold and dark (7 hours of daylight). Spring and autumn are unpredictable.
- **Top attractions:** Tivoli Gardens, Nyhavn, The Little Mermaid, Christiansborg Palace, Rosenborg Castle, Freetown Christiania, Round Tower, Strøget shopping street.
- **Neighborhoods:** Nørrebro (multicultural, street food, nightlife), Vesterbro (trendy, Meatpacking District, craft cocktails), Christianshavn (canals, houseboats, Christiania), Frederiksberg (gardens, zoo, residential elegance), Østerbro (quiet, parks, family-oriented).
- **Local transport:** Cycling is the primary transport — Copenhagen is flat and has excellent bike lanes. Rent a bike (or use city bikes). Metro and S-tog trains with Rejsekort.
- **Food recommendations:** Smørrebrød (open-faced sandwiches), Danish pastries (wienerbrød), hot dogs from pølsevogn street carts, flæskesteg (roast pork), Nordic cuisine (Noma put Copenhagen on the food map). Copenhagen Street Food at Reffen market.
- **Budget tip:** Copenhagen is expensive. Save with Copenhagen Card (transport + 80 attractions). Drink tap water everywhere. Bring food from supermarkets for picnics. Happy-hour deals at bars.
- **Currency:** Danish Krone (DKK). Almost 100% cashless — card/mobile pay everywhere.
- **Language:** Danish. Virtually everyone speaks excellent English.
- **Safety:** Extremely safe. Leave your bike unlocked at a café like locals do (but lock it overnight). Christiania has specific photo rules — respect them.
- **Average daily budget:** DKK 1,000–2,000 mid-range; DKK 500–800 budget.

## Budapest, Hungary
- **Best time to visit:** March–May and September–October. Summer (June–August) is hot and busy. December for Christmas markets (among Europe's best). Winter is cold but thermal baths are perfect.
- **Top attractions:** Hungarian Parliament, Buda Castle, Fisherman's Bastion, Széchenyi Thermal Bath, Ruin Bars (Szimpla Kert), Chain Bridge, St. Stephen's Basilica, Gellért Hill.
- **Neighborhoods:** Jewish Quarter/District VII (ruin bars, street art, nightlife), Castle District (Buda side, views, historic), Belváros/District V (Parliament, riverside, shopping), Újlipótváros/District XIII (local cafés, residential charm), Erzsébetváros (adjacent to Jewish Quarter, alternative scene).
- **Local transport:** BKK passes for metro, trams, and buses. Tram 2 along the Danube is the most scenic public transit ride. Walking across Chain Bridge connects Buda and Pest.
- **Food recommendations:** Goulash (gulyás — soup, not stew), lángos (fried dough with sour cream and cheese), chimney cake (kürtőskalács), chicken paprikash, ruin bar food courts, Great Market Hall for produce and street food. Hungary produces excellent wine — try Tokaji (sweet) or Egri Bikavér (Bull's Blood).
- **Budget tip:** One of Europe's cheapest capitals. Ruin bars are affordable. Thermal baths cost 5,000–8,000 HUF. Free walking tours are excellent. Great Market Hall ground floor has cheap prepared food.
- **Currency:** Hungarian Forint (HUF). Cards widely accepted in tourist areas; carry cash for markets and smaller shops.
- **Language:** Hungarian (one of Europe's hardest languages). English spoken in tourist areas, especially by younger generation.
- **Safety:** Generally safe. Watch for pickpockets on the metro and trams. Scam: overcharging at tourist restaurants (check bill carefully), taxi overcharging (use Bolt app).
- **Average daily budget:** HUF 25,000–50,000 mid-range; HUF 12,000–20,000 budget.

## Dubrovnik, Croatia
- **Best time to visit:** May–June and September–October for warm weather and manageable crowds. July–August is extremely crowded (cruise ship season) and hot. Winter is quiet and mild but some restaurants close.
- **Top attractions:** Old Town walls walk, Stradun (main street), Fort Lovrijenac, cable car to Mount Srđ, Lokrum Island, Rector's Palace, Game of Thrones filming locations.
- **Neighborhoods:** Old Town (within the walls, atmospheric but touristy), Lapad (beach suburb, hotels, more affordable restaurants), Ploče (east of Old Town, luxury, Banje Beach), Gruž (harbor, local market, ferry terminal).
- **Local transport:** Old Town is entirely pedestrian and walkable. Local buses connect suburbs. Ferries to nearby islands (Lokrum, Elaphiti Islands).
- **Food recommendations:** Fresh seafood (grilled fish, black risotto), peka (slow-cooked meat or octopus under a bell), oysters from Ston, pašticada (Dalmatian beef stew), local wine (Plavac Mali red, Pošip white). Eat outside the walls for 30–50% lower prices.
- **Budget tip:** Dubrovnik is Croatia's most expensive city. Buy the Dubrovnik Card for walls + museums + bus. Swim at free beaches (Banje, Šulić). Take the local ferry to Lokrum Island (cheap). Shop at Gruž market for fresh food.
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR, adopted 2023). Cards widely accepted.
- **Language:** Croatian. English widely spoken in tourist areas.
- **Safety:** Very safe. Sunburn and dehydration are the main risks in summer. Cliff jumping at Buža Bar is popular but risky. Sea urchins on rocky beaches — wear water shoes.
- **Average daily budget:** €80–€180 mid-range; €40–€70 budget.

## Reykjavik, Iceland
- **Best time to visit:** June–August for midnight sun, hiking, and warmest weather (10–15°C). September–March for Northern Lights (best October–February). Winter is dark (4 hours daylight in December) but magical.
- **Top attractions:** Blue Lagoon, Golden Circle (Þingvellir, Geysir, Gullfoss), Northern Lights, whale watching, Hallgrímskirkja, Harpa Concert Hall, Perlan museum.
- **Neighborhoods:** Downtown/101 Reykjavik (main street Laugavegur, restaurants, bars, galleries), Grandi (Old Harbour, Whales of Iceland, food trucks), Laugardalur (thermal pool, botanical garden, residential).
- **Local transport:** Strætó bus system. Car rental is essential for Golden Circle and Ring Road. Walking covers downtown Reykjavik in 20 minutes.
- **Food recommendations:** Icelandic lamb soup, plokkfiskur (fish stew), Skyr (Icelandic yogurt), hot dogs at Bæjarins Beztu (institution since 1937), fresh fish at Grandi harbour restaurants. Adventurous: fermented shark (hákarl) and dried fish (harðfiskur).
- **Budget tip:** Iceland is very expensive. Bonus supermarket is the cheapest option for groceries. Tap water is some of the world's best — never buy bottled. Free public thermal pools (Nautholsvik in summer). Cook at your accommodation.
- **Currency:** Icelandic Króna (ISK). 100% cashless — card accepted even at hot dog stands.
- **Language:** Icelandic. Everyone speaks excellent English.
- **Safety:** Extremely safe (often ranked safest country). Natural hazards are the main risk: weather changes rapidly, stay on marked paths near geothermal areas, rogue waves at Reynisfjara black sand beach are dangerous.
- **Average daily budget:** ISK 25,000–50,000 mid-range; ISK 12,000–20,000 budget.

## Singapore
- **Best time to visit:** Year-round destination (consistent 27–32°C). February is driest. November–January has the most rain but also festive celebrations (Christmas on Orchard Road, Chinese New Year). Monsoon showers are short — carry an umbrella.
- **Top attractions:** Marina Bay Sands, Gardens by the Bay, Sentosa Island, Merlion Park, Chinatown, Little India, Orchard Road, Singapore Zoo (night safari).
- **Neighborhoods:** Chinatown (temples, hawker centers, shophouses), Little India (spices, colors, Tekka Market), Kampong Glam/Arab Street (Malay heritage, Sultan Mosque, cafés), Tiong Bahru (art deco flats, cafés, hipster bakeries), Holland Village (expat hangout, bars), Clarke Quay (riverside nightlife).
- **Local transport:** MRT (Mass Rapid Transit) is efficient and air-conditioned. EZ-Link card for MRT and buses. Grab for taxis.
- **Food recommendations:** Hawker centers are Singapore's soul — Michelin-starred chicken rice at Liao Fan Hawker Chan, laksa, char kway teow, roti prata, chili crab (must-try, available at Newton Food Centre or Jumbo Seafood), Hainanese chicken rice. Maxwell Food Centre and Lau Pa Sat are iconic hawker centers.
- **Budget tip:** Hawker center meals cost SGD 3–6 — eat like a local. Gardens by the Bay outdoor gardens are free (Supertree Grove light show at 7:45/8:45 PM is free). Free light and water show at Marina Bay Sands nightly.
- **Currency:** Singapore Dollar (SGD). Cards accepted everywhere. Very cashless.
- **Language:** English (official), Mandarin, Malay, Tamil. English is the common language.
- **Safety:** One of the world's safest cities. Very strict laws — no chewing gum, heavy fines for littering, drug possession carries death penalty. Respect all rules.
- **Average daily budget:** SGD 150–300 mid-range; SGD 60–100 budget.

## Seoul, South Korea
- **Best time to visit:** March–May (cherry blossoms) and September–November (fall foliage). Summer (June–August) is hot, humid, and rainy (monsoon season). Winter (December–February) is bitterly cold but beautiful for snow and fewer tourists.
- **Top attractions:** Gyeongbokgung Palace, Bukchon Hanok Village, Myeongdong, Namsan Tower, Hongdae, DMZ tour, Lotte World, Gangnam district.
- **Neighborhoods:** Hongdae (youth culture, indie music, clubs, street performers), Itaewon (international food, nightlife, diverse), Insadong (traditional crafts, tea houses, galleries), Gangnam (K-pop culture, upscale shopping), Jongno (palaces, traditional Seoul), Ikseon-dong (hanok village turned café district).
- **Local transport:** Metro system is world-class — cheap, fast, clean. T-money card for metro and buses. KTX high-speed rail for day trips.
- **Food recommendations:** Korean BBQ (try samgyeopsal/pork belly in Mapo-gu), bibimbap, kimchi jjigae (stew), tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes from street vendors), fried chicken and beer (chimaek), Korean fried chicken at Kyochon or BHC, street food in Myeongdong and Gwangjang Market (bindaetteok pancakes).
- **Budget tip:** Excellent subway value. Street food in Myeongdong and Gwangjang Market is cheap. Many palaces are under ₩3,000 entry. Free hanbok rental gets you free palace entry. Convenience stores (CU, GS25) have great cheap meals.
- **Currency:** South Korean Won (KRW). Cards accepted almost everywhere. T-money card also works at convenience stores.
- **Language:** Korean. English signage on metro. Limited English outside tourist areas — Papago translation app is better than Google Translate for Korean.
- **Safety:** Extremely safe at all hours. Taxis are safe and cheap late at night. Biggest concern is air pollution (check AQI, especially in spring — fine dust from China).
- **Average daily budget:** ₩80,000–₩180,000 mid-range; ₩40,000–₩70,000 budget.

## Hong Kong, China
- **Best time to visit:** October–December for clear skies and comfortable temperatures (18–25°C). Summer (June–September) is hot, humid, and typhoon season. Chinese New Year (January/February) is festive but many shops close.
- **Top attractions:** Victoria Peak, Star Ferry, Temple Street Night Market, Big Buddha (Tian Tan), Wong Tai Sin Temple, Hong Kong Disneyland, Man Mo Temple, Lantau Island.
- **Neighborhoods:** Central (business hub, elevated walkways, LKF nightlife), Tsim Sha Tsui (harbour views, museums, shopping), Mong Kok (bustling markets, local street food), Sheung Wan (antique shops, dried seafood, cafés), Sai Kung (seafood villages, hiking), Kennedy Town (waterfront, local dining).
- **Local transport:** MTR (metro) is fast and efficient. Octopus card for MTR, buses, ferries, and convenience stores. Star Ferry across Victoria Harbour is iconic and cheap (HKD 3–5). Double-decker trams on Hong Kong Island.
- **Food recommendations:** Dim sum (Tim Ho Wan — cheapest Michelin-starred restaurant), roast goose, wonton noodles, egg waffles, milk tea, congee, dai pai dong (open-air street restaurants). Cha chaan teng (local diners) for cheap Hong Kong-style Western food.
- **Budget tip:** Star Ferry and trams are cheapest transport. Free Symphony of Lights show nightly at 8 PM. Hiking trails (Dragon's Back, Lion Rock) are free. Dai pai dong and cha chaan teng meals cost HKD 40–80. Happy hour drinks in LKF (usually 5–8 PM).
- **Currency:** Hong Kong Dollar (HKD). Cards widely accepted. Octopus card is essential.
- **Language:** Cantonese and English (both official). Mandarin widely understood. English signage everywhere.
- **Safety:** Very safe, even late at night. Typhoon warnings are taken seriously — stay indoors during Signal 8 or above.
- **Average daily budget:** HKD 600–1,500 mid-range; HKD 300–550 budget.

## Hanoi, Vietnam
- **Best time to visit:** October–December (cool and dry) and March–April (spring). Summer (May–August) is hot and humid with heavy rain. January–February can be cold and drizzly.
- **Top attractions:** Hoan Kiem Lake, Old Quarter, Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum, Temple of Literature, Hanoi Train Street, Water Puppet Theatre, Hoa Lo Prison (Hanoi Hilton), Halong Bay (day trip or overnight).
- **Neighborhoods:** Old Quarter (36 streets, each named for its traditional trade — chaos, street food, motorbikes), French Quarter (colonial architecture, Hanoi Opera House, upscale), West Lake (expat area, lakeside cafés, Tran Quoc Pagoda), Ba Dinh (Ho Chi Minh complex, quiet tree-lined streets).
- **Local transport:** Grab (ride-hailing) is essential and cheap. Walking the Old Quarter is an experience. Motorbike taxis for the brave. Local buses are very cheap but confusing. Crossing the street: walk slowly and steadily — traffic will flow around you.
- **Food recommendations:** Pho (try Pho Thin for beef pho with butter), bun cha (grilled pork with noodles — Obama ate at Bun Cha Huong Lien), banh mi (Vietnamese sandwich), egg coffee at Café Giang, bia hoi (fresh draft beer on tiny street chairs for 5,000–10,000 VND), spring rolls.
- **Budget tip:** One of Asia's cheapest capitals. Street food meals cost 30,000–60,000 VND (€1–€2.50). Bia hoi corners sell fresh beer for 5,000 VND. Walking tours of Old Quarter. Halong Bay tours can be booked cheaply through hostels.
- **Currency:** Vietnamese Dong (VND). Cash preferred for street food and small shops. ATMs available. Carry small bills.
- **Language:** Vietnamese. Limited English outside tourist areas. Learn basic phrases — locals appreciate it.
- **Safety:** Generally safe. Traffic is the biggest danger — crossing streets requires courage. Common scams: cyclo (pedicab) riders quoting one price then demanding more, shoe shine "repair" scam, overcharging at restaurants without menus.
- **Average daily budget:** VND 1,000,000–2,500,000 mid-range; VND 400,000–800,000 budget.

## Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia
- **Best time to visit:** Year-round destination (consistent 27–33°C). May–July and December–February are slightly drier. Afternoon thunderstorms are daily — they pass quickly. Ramadan period varies; eating is discreet during daytime but night markets come alive.
- **Top attractions:** Petronas Twin Towers, Batu Caves, KL Tower, Merdeka Square, Chinatown (Petaling Street), Islamic Arts Museum, Thean Hou Temple, Bukit Bintang.
- **Neighborhoods:** Bukit Bintang (shopping, nightlife, Jalan Alor street food), Chinatown/Petaling Street (markets, temples, budget stays), KLCC (Petronas Towers, upscale), Bangsar (expat-friendly, rooftop bars), Chow Kit (wet market, local food, emerging hip scene), Brickfields/Little India (Indian food, temples).
- **Local transport:** RapidKL for LRT, MRT, Monorail, and buses. Touch 'n Go card. Grab is cheap and ubiquitous. KL Sentral is the main transport hub.
- **Food recommendations:** Nasi lemak (coconut rice with sambal — the national dish), roti canai, char kway teow, satay, cendol (iced dessert), banana leaf rice in Brickfields, Jalan Alor street food at night. KL is a food paradise combining Malay, Chinese, Indian, and Nyonya cuisines.
- **Budget tip:** One of Asia's best-value cities. Street food costs RM 5–15. KLCC park and fountains are free. Batu Caves entry is free. Public transport is very cheap. Malls offer air-conditioned relief from the heat for free.
- **Currency:** Malaysian Ringgit (MYR/RM). Cards accepted in malls and restaurants. Carry cash for hawker stalls and markets.
- **Language:** Bahasa Malaysia (Malay). English widely spoken (second language for most). Mandarin and Tamil also common.
- **Safety:** Generally safe. Watch for bag snatching on motorbikes (carry bags on the building side of the sidewalk). Petaling Street has aggressive touts. Avoid unlicensed taxis — use Grab.
- **Average daily budget:** RM 200–500 mid-range; RM 80–150 budget.

## Mumbai, India
- **Best time to visit:** November–February (cool and dry, 20–32°C). March–May is scorching. June–September is monsoon season — dramatic rain, flooded streets, but the city has a unique energy. Diwali (October/November) is spectacular.
- **Top attractions:** Gateway of India, Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, Marine Drive, Elephanta Caves, Dharavi (guided tour — Asia's largest slum, a vibrant micro-economy), Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus, Colaba Causeway, Banganga Tank.
- **Neighborhoods:** Colaba (tourist hub, Gateway, cafés, Causeway market), Bandra (hipster, street art, Bollywood connections, Carter Road), Fort (colonial architecture, Kala Ghoda art district), Juhu (beach, celebrity homes, street food), Lower Parel (converted mills, trendy restaurants, Phoenix Mall).
- **Local transport:** Mumbai local trains are the lifeline (avoid rush hour — seriously). Auto-rickshaws in suburbs. Black-and-yellow taxis in South Mumbai. Ola and Uber apps. Kaali-peeli (black-yellow) taxis use meters — insist on it.
- **Food recommendations:** Vada pav (Mumbai's signature street snack — spiced potato fritter in bread), pav bhaji, bhel puri at Chowpatty Beach, Parsi cuisine at Britannia & Co (berry pulao), dosas at Ram Ashray, kebabs at Mohammad Ali Road (evening). Mumbai's street food scene is legendary.
- **Budget tip:** Street food costs ₹20–₹100. Local trains are incredibly cheap (₹5–₹15). Free walking tours of heritage areas. Gateway of India and Marine Drive are free. Bargain hard at Colaba Causeway.
- **Currency:** Indian Rupee (INR). Cards accepted at restaurants and shops. Cash essential for street food, rickshaws, and small shops. UPI (Google Pay) is widespread among locals.
- **Language:** Hindi and Marathi. English widely spoken, especially in South Mumbai.
- **Safety:** Generally safe for tourists. Traffic is chaotic. Be cautious of pickpockets in crowded trains and markets. Scam: taxi/rickshaw "broken meter" — use Ola/Uber. Avoid drinking tap water.
- **Average daily budget:** ₹3,000–₹8,000 mid-range; ₹1,000–₹2,500 budget.

## Taipei, Taiwan
- **Best time to visit:** October–December (dry, 20–27°C) and March–May (spring). Summer (June–September) is hot, humid, and typhoon season. Chinese New Year (January/February) is festive but some shops close.
- **Top attractions:** Taipei 101, National Palace Museum, Longshan Temple, Elephant Mountain (sunset hike), Chiang Kai-shek Memorial, Jiufen (day trip), Beitou Hot Springs, Yangmingshan National Park.
- **Neighborhoods:** Da'an (tree-lined streets, best restaurants, Yongkang Street), Ximending (youth culture, shopping, neon), Zhongshan (art, Japanese-era buildings, cafés), Datong (Dihua Street, traditional shops, fabric market), Songshan (Raohe Night Market, temples).
- **Local transport:** MRT (metro) is excellent, clean, and cheap. EasyCard for MRT, buses, bikes, and convenience stores. YouBike (bike share) covers the city. Uber and taxis are cheap.
- **Food recommendations:** Night markets are essential — Shilin (biggest), Raohe (best for food), Ningxia (local favourite). Must-try: beef noodle soup, xiao long bao (soup dumplings at Din Tai Fung — originated here), stinky tofu, bubble tea (invented in Taiwan), oyster omelette, gua bao (pork belly bun), scallion pancakes.
- **Budget tip:** Night market food costs TWD 50–150 per dish. MRT is very cheap. National Palace Museum is TWD 350. Hiking trails (Elephant Mountain, Yangmingshan) are free. Convenience stores (7-Eleven, FamilyMart) have great cheap meals.
- **Currency:** New Taiwan Dollar (TWD). Cards accepted in most shops. Cash needed for night markets and small eateries.
- **Language:** Mandarin Chinese. English is limited but MRT has English signage. Younger people may speak some English.
- **Safety:** Extremely safe at all hours. One of the safest cities in Asia. Typhoon warnings should be taken seriously.
- **Average daily budget:** TWD 2,500–5,500 mid-range; TWD 1,200–2,000 budget.

## Mexico City, Mexico
- **Best time to visit:** March–May (dry, warm) and October–November (pleasant, Día de los Muertos). June–September is rainy (afternoon showers). December–February is dry and cool. Altitude (2,240m) means cooler than expected.
- **Top attractions:** Zócalo, Palacio de Bellas Artes, Chapultepec Castle, Frida Kahlo Museum (Casa Azul), Templo Mayor, Teotihuacán pyramids (day trip), Coyoacán, Xochimilco floating gardens.
- **Neighborhoods:** Roma (hipster cafés, art deco architecture, foodie capital), Condesa (tree-lined avenues, parks, brunch culture), Coyoacán (Frida Kahlo's village, colorful markets), Centro Histórico (Zócalo, colonial architecture, museums), Polanco (upscale, world-class restaurants, Chapultepec), San Rafael (emerging, affordable, local).
- **Local transport:** Metro is extensive and incredibly cheap (MXN 5 per ride). Metrobús for key routes. Uber is safe and affordable. Walking is great in Roma/Condesa but not across the whole city.
- **Food recommendations:** Tacos al pastor (best at El Huequito or any street stand), mole (Oaxacan restaurants in Roma), chilaquiles for breakfast, tamales from morning street vendors, elote (grilled corn), churros at El Moro, mezcal bars in Roma/Condesa. Street food taco stands with long local lines are the ones to trust.
- **Budget tip:** One of the world's best food cities at incredibly low prices. Tacos cost MXN 15–30. Metro rides MXN 5. Many museums are free on Sundays. Chapultepec Park and its museums are excellent free/cheap options.
- **Currency:** Mexican Peso (MXN). Cards accepted in Roma/Condesa/Polanco. Cash for street food, markets, and taxis. ATMs in banks (not convenience stores) for best rates.
- **Language:** Spanish. Limited English outside tourist areas. Basic Spanish is very helpful.
- **Safety:** Exercise caution. Stick to Roma, Condesa, Polanco, Coyoacán, and Centro Histórico (daytime). Use Uber instead of street taxis (especially at night). Don't flash expensive jewelry or electronics. The city is generally safe in tourist neighborhoods but research areas before wandering.
- **Average daily budget:** MXN 1,500–3,500 mid-range; MXN 600–1,200 budget.

## Buenos Aires, Argentina
- **Best time to visit:** March–May (autumn, mild) and September–November (spring). Summer (December–February) is hot and humid; many locals leave in January. Winter (June–August) is cool but pleasant.
- **Top attractions:** La Boca/Caminito, Recoleta Cemetery, Teatro Colón, Plaza de Mayo, San Telmo Market (Sunday), MALBA museum, Palermo parks and lakes, tango show.
- **Neighborhoods:** Palermo (largest barrio — Palermo Soho for boutiques and cafés, Palermo Hollywood for restaurants and bars), San Telmo (antiques, tango in the streets, Sunday market), Recoleta (elegant, Parisian-style, cemetery and museums), La Boca (colorful but stick to tourist area), Belgrano (quiet, Chinatown, residential), Villa Crespo (emerging foodie area, leather shops).
- **Local transport:** SUBE card for subte (subway), buses (colectivos). Buses cover the entire city. Walking is great in Palermo and San Telmo. Uber works but is technically grey-area (locals use it anyway).
- **Food recommendations:** Asado (Argentine BBQ — try parilla restaurants), empanadas (try different fillings), milanesa a la napolitana, dulce de leche everything, medialunas (croissants), choripán (chorizo sandwich). Buenos Aires is a steak capital — order bife de chorizo. Malbec wine is world-class and cheap.
- **Budget tip:** Blue dollar rate means USD cash gets significantly better exchange rates at "cuevas" (informal exchanges) than official bank rate. Steak dinners are a fraction of European prices. Free walking tours. San Telmo Sunday market is free to browse. Subte rides are very cheap.
- **Currency:** Argentine Peso (ARS). Cash is king — bring USD or EUR to exchange. Blue dollar rate fluctuates; check daily. Credit cards get the official (worse) rate. Inflation is very high — prices change frequently.
- **Language:** Spanish (Rioplatense dialect with Italian influence). Limited English. Basic Spanish is very helpful.
- **Safety:** Generally safe in tourist areas. Petty theft and pickpocketing in crowded places. Don't wander into La Boca beyond the tourist streets. Use ATMs inside banks. Avoid flashing valuables.
- **Average daily budget:** $40–$100 USD mid-range; $20–$40 USD budget (at blue dollar rate; prices shift with inflation).

## Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
- **Best time to visit:** May–October (dry season, 20–27°C). December–March is hot and humid with afternoon rain. Carnival (February/March) is iconic but hotels are 3x the price. September–October is ideal: dry, warm, fewer crowds.
- **Top attractions:** Christ the Redeemer (Corcovado), Sugarloaf Mountain, Copacabana Beach, Ipanema Beach, Lapa Arches, Selaron Steps, Tijuca Forest, Maracanã Stadium.
- **Neighborhoods:** Ipanema (upscale beach, restaurants, safe), Copacabana (iconic beach, busy, more affordable), Leblon (quietest of the beach neighborhoods, local favourite), Santa Teresa (bohemian hill, art studios, cobblestone streets), Lapa (nightlife, samba clubs, arches), Botafogo (local dining, view of Sugarloaf).
- **Local transport:** Metro connects main tourist areas. Uber is essential and cheap. Buses are complicated for tourists. Don't drive — traffic is brutal.
- **Food recommendations:** Feijoada (black bean and pork stew — traditional Saturday lunch), açaí bowls (served with granola on the beach), pastel (fried pastry), coxinha (chicken croquette), churrascaria rodízio (all-you-can-eat BBQ), caipirinhas (cachaça cocktail). Juice bars serve fresh tropical fruit juices for cheap.
- **Budget tip:** Beaches are free and beautiful. Hiking Tijuca Forest is free. Sugarloaf and Christ the Redeemer tickets should be bought online. Happy hour caipirinha specials at beach kiosks. Kilo restaurants charge by weight — budget-friendly lunch option.
- **Currency:** Brazilian Real (BRL). Cards accepted in most restaurants. Cash for beach vendors, small shops. ATMs in banks.
- **Language:** Portuguese (not Spanish). Limited English. Learn basic Portuguese phrases — "obrigado/a" (thank you) goes a long way.
- **Safety:** Exercise caution. Don't bring valuables to the beach (phone, wallet). Don't wear expensive jewelry. Avoid favelas unless on a guided tour with a community-based operator. Use Uber at night instead of walking. Ipanema and Leblon are safest. Stay aware of surroundings.
- **Average daily budget:** BRL 300–700 mid-range; BRL 120–250 budget.

## Lima, Peru
- **Best time to visit:** December–March for sunshine (summer, 25–30°C). May–November is grey and overcast (garúa mist) but still mild (15–19°C). No rain — Lima is a desert city.
- **Top attractions:** Miraflores (cliff-top boardwalk), Barranco (bohemian district), Plaza Mayor, Larco Museum, Huaca Pucllana (pre-Inca pyramid), Pachacamac ruins, food tours.
- **Neighborhoods:** Miraflores (tourist hub, safe, oceanfront parks, restaurants), Barranco (art galleries, street art, nightlife, Bridge of Sighs), San Isidro (upscale, parks, fine dining), Centro Histórico (colonial architecture, churches, busy), Surquillo (local market, emerging foodie area).
- **Local transport:** Metropolitano (BRT bus), Lima Metro Line 1. Uber and DiDi are essential and cheap. Traffic is terrible — allow extra time.
- **Food recommendations:** Lima is South America's culinary capital. Ceviche (must-try — La Mar or a mercado), lomo saltado (stir-fried beef with fries), anticuchos (grilled beef hearts from street carts), causa (layered potato dish), picarones (sweet potato doughnuts). Pisco sour is the national cocktail.
- **Budget tip:** Menu del día at local restaurants offers 2-3 courses for PEN 10–15. Mercado de Surquillo has cheap, fresh ceviche. Walking around Miraflores and Barranco is free. Free guided tours of Centro Histórico.
- **Currency:** Peruvian Sol (PEN). Cards accepted in Miraflores/Barranco. Cash for markets and street food.
- **Language:** Spanish. English spoken in tourist restaurants and hotels in Miraflores.
- **Safety:** Miraflores and Barranco are safe. Be cautious in Centro Histórico, especially at night. Use registered taxis or Uber. Don't flash electronics in crowded areas. Express kidnapping (rare but serious) — avoid unlicensed taxis.
- **Average daily budget:** PEN 200–500 mid-range; PEN 80–150 budget.

## San Francisco, USA
- **Best time to visit:** September–October (Indian summer, warmest weather). Summer is cool and foggy (Mark Twain's famous quote applies). Spring and fall are pleasant. Winter is mild and rainy.
- **Top attractions:** Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, Fisherman's Wharf, cable cars, Chinatown, Painted Ladies, Golden Gate Park, Lombard Street.
- **Neighborhoods:** Mission District (murals, taquerias, nightlife), North Beach (Little Italy, City Lights bookstore, Beat poetry), Haight-Ashbury (counterculture history, vintage shops), Castro (LGBTQ+ history, vibrant), SoMa (museums, tech offices, nightlife), Hayes Valley (boutiques, cafés).

- **Language:** English. Highly multilingual.

## Toronto, Canada
- **Best time to visit:** June–September for warm weather (20–30°C) and festivals (TIFF in September, Pride in June). Fall foliage in October is beautiful. Winter (December–March) is cold (down to -15°C) but PATH underground network keeps you warm.
- **Top attractions:** CN Tower, Royal Ontario Museum, Distillery District, St. Lawrence Market, Toronto Islands, Kensington Market, Art Gallery of Ontario, Ripley's Aquarium.
- **Neighborhoods:** Kensington Market (eclectic, vintage, multicultural food stalls), Queen West (street art, galleries, indie shops), Distillery District (Victorian industrial, galleries, restaurants), Yorkville (upscale shopping, high-end dining), Chinatown (Spadina Avenue, cheap eats), Leslieville (brunch, local boutiques).
- **Local transport:** TTC (subway, streetcars, buses) with PRESTO card. Streetcars on King and Queen are scenic. Uber and Lyft available. Bike Share Toronto for cycling.
- **Food recommendations:** Peameal bacon sandwich at St. Lawrence Market (Carousel Bakery), poutine, dim sum in Markham/Chinatown, patties (Jamaican patty from Patty King), multicultural food in Kensington Market. Toronto's diversity means world-class Ethiopian, Korean, Portuguese, Vietnamese, and Indian food.
- **Budget tip:** Many museums have free or discounted evenings (AGO is free Wednesday evenings). Toronto Islands ferry is cheap. Kensington Market food is affordable. PATH has free shelter in winter. High Park is free.

- **Language:** English and French (officially bilingual country). Toronto is one of the world's most multicultural cities.

## Cape Town, South Africa
- **Best time to visit:** November–March (summer, 25–30°C, long days). December–January is peak season. April–May (autumn) is quieter and still pleasant. June–August is winter (rainy, 10–18°C) but whale-watching season. September–October is wildflower season.
- **Top attractions:** Table Mountain, Cape Point, Robben Island, V&A Waterfront, Kirstenbosch Botanical Gardens, Camps Bay Beach, Bo-Kaap (colorful houses), Cape Winelands (Stellenbosch, Franschhoek day trips).
- **Neighborhoods:** City Bowl (CBD, Long Street nightlife, restaurants), Bo-Kaap (Cape Malay heritage, colorful, cooking classes), Woodstock (street art, Old Biscuit Mill market), Camps Bay (beach, sundowners, upscale), Sea Point (promenade, diverse dining), Observatory (student area, eclectic, affordable).
- **Local transport:** MyCiTi bus for main routes. Uber is the primary transport for tourists (cheap and safe). Car rental recommended for Cape Point and winelands. Avoid minibus taxis as a tourist.
- **Food recommendations:** Braai (South African BBQ), bobotie (Cape Malay curried mince), bunny chow, biltong (dried cured meat), Cape Malay curry in Bo-Kaap, seafood at Kalk Bay harbour, wine tasting in Stellenbosch. South African wine is excellent and affordable.
- **Budget tip:** Table Mountain hike (Platteklip Gorge) is free if you walk up. Beach access is free. Free walking tours of Bo-Kaap and city center. Wine tasting is cheap (ZAR 50–100 for a flight). Braai at your accommodation saves on dining.
- **Currency:** South African Rand (ZAR). Cards accepted most places. Cash for markets and small shops.
- **Language:** English, Afrikaans, Xhosa (11 official languages). English widely spoken everywhere.
- **Safety:** Exercise significant caution. Don't walk alone at night, even in tourist areas. Use Uber instead of walking after dark. Don't leave valuables visible in cars. Avoid isolated areas and certain townships without a guide. Camps Bay, Waterfront, and City Bowl are safest. Despite caution needed, millions visit safely each year.
- **Average daily budget:** ZAR 1,500–3,500 mid-range; ZAR 600–1,200 budget.

## Marrakech, Morocco
- **Best time to visit:** March–May and October–November (20–28°C). Summer (June–August) is extremely hot (40°C+). Winter (December–February) is mild during the day but cold at night. Ramadan timing varies — plan accordingly.
- **Top attractions:** Jemaa el-Fna square, Bahia Palace, Majorelle Garden, Koutoubia Mosque, Saadian Tombs, Medina souks, Ben Youssef Madrasa, Atlas Mountains (day trip).
- **Neighborhoods:** Medina (ancient walled city, riads, souks — this is where you want to stay), Gueliz (French colonial new town, modern restaurants, galleries), Kasbah (near royal palace, quieter section of Medina), Mellah (former Jewish quarter, spice market).
- **Local transport:** Walking is the only option in the Medina (no cars in narrow alleys). Petit taxis (beige, metered) for getting to Gueliz. Horse-drawn carriages (calèches) for atmosphere. Arrange day trips through your riad.
- **Food recommendations:** Tagine (slow-cooked stew — lamb with prunes, chicken with preserved lemons), couscous (traditional Friday dish), pastilla (sweet-savory pigeon pie), harira (lentil soup), msemen (flatbread), mint tea (don't refuse — it's hospitality). Jemaa el-Fna food stalls come alive at sunset.
- **Budget tip:** Riads (traditional courtyard houses) offer beautiful stays from MAD 300–500/night. Street food at Jemaa el-Fna is cheap (MAD 30–60). Haggle in souks — start at 30% of asking price. Rooftop terraces with Atlas views at many cafés.
- **Currency:** Moroccan Dirham (MAD). Cash is essential — ATMs in Gueliz and Medina edges. Cards accepted at upscale restaurants and hotels only.
- **Language:** Arabic (Darija dialect), French, Berber. English in tourist areas. French is very useful.
- **Safety:** Generally safe but overwhelming for first-time visitors. Be firm but polite declining touts and "guides." Scams: fake guides leading you to shops (you'll get lost in the Medina — that's normal, use Google Maps), henna artists grabbing your hand then demanding payment, monkey handlers wanting money for photos. Women may experience catcalling — dress modestly.
- **Average daily budget:** MAD 600–1,500 mid-range; MAD 250–500 budget.

## Cairo, Egypt
- **Best time to visit:** October–April (20–28°C). Summer (May–September) is brutally hot (35–45°C) and dusty. December–February can be surprisingly cool (10–20°C), especially at night. Ramadan affects restaurant hours but the city comes alive after sunset.
- **Top attractions:** Pyramids of Giza and Sphinx, Egyptian Museum (and new Grand Egyptian Museum), Khan el-Khalili bazaar, Coptic Cairo, Al-Azhar Mosque, Citadel of Saladin, Nile felucca ride, Saqqara (day trip).
- **Neighborhoods:** Downtown (Tahrir Square, Egyptian Museum, colonial-era architecture), Zamalek (island district, embassies, upscale restaurants, quieter), Islamic Cairo (medieval mosques, Khan el-Khalili), Coptic Cairo (historic churches, oldest part of the city), Maadi (expat suburb, restaurants, tree-lined streets), Giza (near Pyramids, touristic).
- **Local transport:** Cairo Metro (cheapest transport, women-only cars available). Uber and Careem are essential — avoid street taxis unless you speak Arabic and know the fare. Traffic is legendary — allow double the expected time for any journey.
- **Food recommendations:** Koshari (national dish — lentils, rice, pasta, tomato sauce, fried onions), ful medames (fava bean breakfast), taameya (Egyptian falafel — made with fava not chickpea), shawarma, molokhia (green soup), grilled kebab and kofta, fresh mango and sugarcane juice from street vendors.
- **Budget tip:** One of the cheapest major tourist destinations. Street food costs EGP 20–50. Pyramids entry EGP 200 (foreigners). Haggle hard at Khan el-Khalili. Felucca rides at sunset are cheap (EGP 100–200/hour). Downtown walking tours cover major sites.
- **Currency:** Egyptian Pound (EGP). Cash is essential for most places. ATMs available but carry backup cash.
- **Language:** Arabic (Egyptian dialect). English spoken at hotels and tourist sites. Very limited elsewhere.
- **Safety:** Generally safe for tourists at major sites. Aggressive touts at the Pyramids (hire an official guide). Scams: "the museum is closed" (to redirect you to a shop), camel ride price switching (agree beforehand, in writing), fake antiques at Khan el-Khalili. Dress modestly, especially women. Traffic is the biggest real danger.
- **Average daily budget:** EGP 2,000–5,000 mid-range; EGP 700–1,500 budget.

## Nairobi, Kenya
- **Best time to visit:** June–October (dry season, 15–25°C, best for safari). January–February is also dry and warm. March–May is the long rains. November has short rains. The Great Migration in Masai Mara peaks July–October.
- **Top attractions:** Nairobi National Park (safari with city skyline backdrop), David Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage, Giraffe Centre, Karen Blixen Museum, Nairobi National Museum, day trips to Masai Mara, Lake Nakuru, or Amboseli.
- **Neighborhoods:** Westlands (nightlife, restaurants, malls), Karen (leafy suburb, Giraffe Centre, Karen Blixen), Kilimani (restaurants, mid-range hotels), CBD (central business, markets, busy), Ngong Road area (cafés, residential).
- **Local transport:** Uber and Bolt are the recommended transport. Matatus (minibuses) are the local way but chaotic for tourists. SGR train for Mombasa trips. Walking is fine in Westlands and Karen during the day.
- **Food recommendations:** Nyama choma (grilled meat — try Carnivore restaurant), ugali with sukuma wiki (cornmeal with greens), chapati, samosas (Indian influence), mandazi (doughnut), Kenyan tea and coffee (world-class). Java House chain is great for coffee.
- **Budget tip:** Street food is very cheap. Nairobi National Park is the cheapest safari option near the city. Book safaris from Nairobi for better prices than international operators. Matatu rides are pennies (KES 30–50).
- **Currency:** Kenyan Shilling (KES). M-Pesa (mobile money) is ubiquitous — get a local SIM for convenience. Cash for markets. Cards at restaurants and hotels.
- **Language:** Swahili and English (both official). English widely spoken.
- **Safety:** Exercise caution. Don't walk around at night. Use Uber/Bolt, not street taxis. Keep phone and valuables discreet — phone snatching is common. Avoid CBD after dark. Westlands and Karen are safest. Safari vehicles and organized tours are safe.
- **Average daily budget:** KES 8,000–20,000 mid-range; KES 3,000–6,000 budget.

## Amman, Jordan
- **Best time to visit:** March–May and September–November (20–28°C). Summer is hot and dry (35°C+). Winter is cool to cold (5–15°C) with occasional rain. Combine with Petra and Wadi Rum trips.
- **Top attractions:** Citadel, Roman Theatre, Rainbow Street, King Abdullah Mosque, Jordan Museum, day trips to Petra, Wadi Rum, Dead Sea, Jerash.
- **Neighborhoods:** Rainbow Street/Jabal Amman (historic, cafés, galleries, sunset views), Downtown/Al-Balad (Roman Theatre, souks, budget eats, authentic), Abdoun (upscale, restaurants, nightlife), Sweifieh (malls, modern dining), Shmeisani (business district, hotels).
- **Local transport:** Uber and Careem are essential. Public buses exist but are confusing. Rent a car for day trips to Petra, Wadi Rum, and Dead Sea. Jordan Pass includes visa, Petra, and 40+ attractions — essential purchase.
- **Food recommendations:** Mansaf (national dish — lamb with fermented yogurt and rice), falafel, hummus, knafeh (sweet cheese pastry — try Habibah on Rainbow Street), shawarma, maqluba (upside-down rice and chicken). Arabic coffee and tea are part of every interaction.
- **Budget tip:** Jordan Pass (JOD 70–80) saves significantly — covers visa fee + Petra + sites. Street food is very cheap (JOD 0.50–1.50 for falafel). Amman is cheaper than Petra-area hotels. Wadi Rum Bedouin camps are affordable and unforgettable.
- **Currency:** Jordanian Dinar (JOD). One of the highest-valued currencies. Cash preferred for small shops and markets. Cards at hotels and restaurants.
- **Language:** Arabic. English widely spoken, especially in tourist areas and by younger Jordanians. People are exceptionally hospitable.
- **Safety:** Very safe. Jordan is one of the safest countries in the Middle East. Jordanians are famously welcoming. Dress modestly outside tourist hotels. Women may want to cover shoulders and knees.
- **Average daily budget:** JOD 40–90 mid-range; JOD 20–35 budget.

## Auckland, New Zealand

## Kyoto, Japan
- **Best time to visit:** March–May (cherry blossoms peak late March to mid-April) and October–November (spectacular autumn foliage). Summer is hot and humid. Winter is cold but atmospheric — fewer tourists, stunning snow-covered temples.
- **Top attractions:** Fushimi Inari Shrine (1,000 torii gates), Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, Kiyomizu-dera Temple, Nijo Castle, Gion District (geisha district), Philosopher's Path.
- **Neighborhoods:** Higashiyama (temples, traditional streets, tea houses), Gion (geisha culture, historic, evening atmosphere), Arashiyama (bamboo, monkey park, scenic river), Downtown/Kawaramachi (shopping, restaurants, nightlife), Nishiki Market area (food market, kitchen of Kyoto).
- **Local transport:** Bus system is efficient — get a bus day pass. JR trains to Arashiyama and Fushimi Inari. Rent a bicycle — Kyoto is flat and perfect for cycling. Walk the eastern Higashiyama temple path.
- **Food recommendations:** Kaiseki (multi-course traditional meal), matcha everything (Kyoto is Japan's tea capital), yudofu (hot tofu — especially in Arashiyama), obanzai (Kyoto home-style cooking), Nishiki Market for street snacks, ramen at Ippudo. Kyoto wagashi (traditional sweets) with matcha at a tea house.
- **Budget tip:** Many temple gardens charge ¥300–¥600 entry but the experience is worth it. Nishiki Market snacking is cheaper than restaurants. Fushimi Inari is free and open 24/7 (go at dawn to avoid crowds). Cycle instead of taking buses. Lunch sets are much cheaper than dinner.
- **Currency:** Japanese Yen (JPY). More cash-dependent than Tokyo. Carry yen.
- **Language:** Japanese. Less English spoken than Tokyo — have Google Translate ready.
- **Safety:** Extremely safe. Follow etiquette: don't photograph geishas without permission in Gion, stay on temple paths, remove shoes where indicated.
- **Average daily budget:** ¥10,000–¥20,000 mid-range; ¥5,000–¥9,000 budget.

## Cartagena, Colombia
- **Best time to visit:** December–April (dry season). July–August are also relatively dry. May–June and September–November have rain. Year-round warm (28–32°C and humid). Avoid Semana Santa (Easter week) unless you want crowds.
- **Top attractions:** Walled City (Ciudad Amurallada), Castillo San Felipe de Barajas, Getsemaní street art, Rosario Islands (boat trip), San Pedro Claver Church, Torre del Reloj, sunset from Café del Mar on the walls.
- **Neighborhoods:** Old Town/Centro Histórico (colonial architecture, plazas, upscale dining), Getsemaní (artsy, street art, backpacker bars, emerging), Bocagrande (beach highrise strip, modern), San Diego (quieter old town section, boutique hotels), Manga (local residential, emerging restaurants).

- **Food recommendations:** Ceviche, arepas de huevo (egg-stuffed corn cakes), cazuela de mariscos (seafood stew), coconut rice, fried fish at La Cevichería, fresh fruit from palenqueras (fruit vendors in colorful dress), limonada de coco (coconut limeade). Street food in Getsemaní plazas at night.
- **Budget tip:** Walking the walls at sunset is free. Getsemaní has cheaper food and accommodation than Old Town. Playa Blanca day trips can be arranged cheaply. Street food arepas and jugos (juices) cost COP 3,000–8,000. Avoid restaurants with laminated menus in English — they're tourist traps.

- **Average daily budget:** COP 200,000–500,000 mid-range; COP 80,000–150,000 budget.

## Kathmandu, Nepal
- **Best time to visit:** October–November (post-monsoon, clear skies, perfect for trekking and mountain views) and March–April (spring, rhododendron blooms). December–February is cold but clear. June–September is monsoon (heavy rain, leeches on trails, but Mustang/Upper regions are dry).
- **Top attractions:** Boudhanath Stupa, Swayambhunath (Monkey Temple), Durbar Square, Pashupatinath Temple, Thamel district, Patan, Bhaktapur (day trip), gateway to Everest Base Camp and Annapurna treks.
- **Neighborhoods:** Thamel (tourist hub, trekking gear shops, restaurants, noisy), Patan/Lalitpur (ancient city, Durbar Square, artisan workshops, quieter), Boudha (around Boudhanath, Tibetan culture, monasteries, peaceful), Jhamsikhel (upscale cafés, expat area), Freak Street (old hippie trail, local feel, cheap).
- **Local transport:** Walking in Thamel and old city areas. Local buses are cheap but overcrowded. Taxis (negotiate fare before riding) or Pathao/inDrive apps. Renting a scooter is possible but traffic is chaotic. Domestic flights for trekking regions.
- **Food recommendations:** Dal bhat (lentil soup with rice — the national meal, unlimited refills), momos (dumplings — try buff/buffalo momos), thukpa (Tibetan noodle soup), sel roti (sweet rice doughnut), Newari cuisine in Patan and Bhaktapur (choyla, yomari), masala tea. "Dal bhat power, 24 hour" is the local saying.
- **Budget tip:** One of the cheapest destinations in the world. Dal bhat costs NPR 200–400. Guesthouses from NPR 500–1,500. Trekking permits and TIMS cards are mandatory but affordable. Hire a local guide — it's cheap and supports the economy. Bhaktapur day trip with Durbar Square combo ticket.
- **Currency:** Nepali Rupee (NPR). Cash is essential — many places don't accept cards. ATMs available in Thamel (max withdrawal NPR 35,000 with fees). Bring USD to exchange for trekking regions.
- **Language:** Nepali. English spoken in Thamel and by trekking guides. "Namaste" (hello) is universal.
- **Safety:** Generally safe and people are incredibly friendly. Altitude sickness is the main risk when trekking — acclimatize properly. Stray dogs are common. Traffic is chaotic with no rules. Air pollution in Kathmandu is severe — bring a mask. Earthquake-prone region — know building exit routes.
- **Average daily budget:** NPR 4,000–10,000 mid-range; NPR 1,500–3,500 budget.
