# Currency, Cash, and Card Acceptance by Destination

Use these notes as a planning guide. Exchange rates move daily; tipping norms and card acceptance shift as destinations digitalize. Always cross-check with a recent source close to departure.

- Most destinations penalize non-local currency ATM withdrawals with a dynamic currency conversion (DCC) prompt — always choose to be charged in the local currency.
- Notify your card issuer of international travel to reduce the chance of fraud blocks, or use a travel-friendly card with no foreign transaction fees.
- Keep a small reserve of local cash for taxis, tips, and small vendors, even in card-friendly destinations.

## France (Schengen Area)
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR, €).
- **Cash vs card:** Card dominant, including for small purchases. Contactless widely supported.
- **ATMs:** Plentiful. Bank ATMs avoid the worst fees; avoid Euronet machines where possible.
- **Tipping:** Service is included by law. Rounding up or leaving €1–2 for good service in cafes is polite, not expected. 5–10% for excellent restaurant service.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard near-universal. American Express accepted in upscale venues only. Some small bakeries and markets still cash-only with €5–10 minimums.

## Japan
- **Currency:** Japanese yen (JPY, ¥). 100 yen ≈ USD 0.65 (rates fluctuate).
- **Cash vs card:** Cash still widely used, especially at small restaurants, temples, and rural inns. Tokyo and Osaka increasingly card-friendly, but carry cash.
- **ATMs:** 7-Eleven, Japan Post (Yucho), and Family Mart ATMs reliably accept foreign cards. Bank ATMs often do not.
- **Tipping:** Not customary and can cause confusion or offense. A small gift or simply saying "gochisousama deshita" is appropriate.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa, Mastercard, JCB most common. IC travel cards (Suica, Pasmo) are useful for transit and convenience stores.

## United States
- **Currency:** US dollar (USD, $).
- **Cash vs card:** Card-dominant. Most venues accept cards, contactless, Apple Pay, and Google Pay.
- **ATMs:** Everywhere. Non-bank ATMs charge $3–6 fees on top of your issuer's fees.
- **Tipping:** Extensive and often expected: 18–22% at sit-down restaurants, $1–2 per drink at bars, 15–20% for taxis and rideshares, $2–5 per night for housekeeping, $1–2 per bag for porters.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard universal. American Express widely accepted but not at every small venue. Discover mostly domestic.
- **Special note:** Tipping is part of service workers' income due to sub-minimum tipped wages in many states.

## Thailand
- **Currency:** Thai baht (THB, ฿).
- **Cash vs card:** Mixed. Cash still common at street food, markets, and tuk-tuks; shopping malls, mid-range restaurants, and hotels take cards.
- **ATMs:** Widely available. Fee of THB 150–220 per withdrawal for foreign cards is standard — withdraw larger amounts less often.
- **Tipping:** Not traditional but appreciated. Rounding up taxis, THB 20–50 per meal or massage is generous enough.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard widely taken; many small vendors add a 3% surcharge.

## Spain (Schengen Area)
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR, €).
- **Cash vs card:** Card-friendly in cities; rural tapas bars may still be cash-preferred.
- **ATMs:** Bank ATMs widely available. Avoid the Euronet standalone machines.
- **Tipping:** Not customary. Rounding up the bill or leaving 5% for excellent service is enough. Many locals leave no tip.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard near-universal.

## UAE (Dubai)
- **Currency:** UAE dirham (AED). Pegged at ~3.67 AED per USD.
- **Cash vs card:** Card-friendly everywhere; cash still useful at souks and taxis.
- **ATMs:** Plentiful. ATM fees are relatively modest.
- **Tipping:** 10–15% in restaurants if service is not included; AED 5–10 for taxi drivers, AED 5 per bag for porters.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa, Mastercard, and Apple Pay widely accepted.

## Italy (Schengen Area)
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR, €).
- **Cash vs card:** Card acceptance has expanded; vendors are legally required to accept electronic payment. Small trattorie in villages may still prefer cash.
- **ATMs:** Bank ATMs (bancomat) widespread and reasonable in fees.
- **Tipping:** Service (servizio) sometimes included. Coperto (cover charge) is not a tip. Leave €1–2 or round up for good service; 5–10% at fine dining.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard universal.

## Germany (Schengen Area)
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR, €).
- **Cash vs card:** Cash still more dominant than in France or Spain. Many small bakeries, restaurants, and even some mid-size shops are cash-only or require EC card (Girocard), not credit.
- **ATMs:** Common. Use bank ATMs (Sparkasse, Deutsche Bank) to avoid fees.
- **Tipping:** 5–10% by telling the server the rounded total when paying, rather than leaving cash on the table.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa/Mastercard common in tourist areas but not assumed elsewhere. Carry cash in Berlin, Munich, and smaller cities.

## Netherlands (Schengen Area)
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR, €).
- **Cash vs card:** Very card-friendly, but many venues accept only Maestro/V Pay (local European debit), not international credit cards. Check before ordering.
- **ATMs:** Plentiful.
- **Tipping:** Round up or leave 5–10% for good service.
- **Card acceptance:** Contactless near-universal; carry a debit card with Maestro support where possible.

## Singapore
- **Currency:** Singapore dollar (SGD).
- **Cash vs card:** Highly card- and contactless-friendly. Hawker centres increasingly accept PayNow / QR codes; some stalls still cash-only.
- **ATMs:** Everywhere.
- **Tipping:** Not customary. A 10% service charge and 9% GST are already added at most restaurants.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa, Mastercard, Amex near-universal; Apple Pay, Google Pay, and PayNow widely accepted.

## India
- **Currency:** Indian rupee (INR, ₹).
- **Cash vs card:** Mixed. UPI (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm) dominates urban payments but usually requires an Indian bank account. Cash still vital for markets, rickshaws, and rural travel.
- **ATMs:** Widely available. Some have foreign-card withdrawal limits (often INR 10,000 per transaction).
- **Tipping:** 10% at mid- to upper-range restaurants if service charge is not included. INR 50–100 for porters; INR 100 per day for drivers is generous.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard accepted at hotels, malls, chain restaurants. Many small businesses card-free.
- **Special note:** Importing or exporting INR is restricted for non-residents. Exchange at the airport or RBI-authorized outlets; keep receipts for reconversion.

## Indonesia (Bali)
- **Currency:** Indonesian rupiah (IDR).
- **Cash vs card:** Cash dominant outside hotels and upmarket restaurants. IDR 100,000 and IDR 50,000 notes are the most useful denominations.
- **ATMs:** Common in tourist areas. Skimming has been an issue — use ATMs attached to banks during business hours when possible.
- **Tipping:** 10% service charge often included. A small rounding tip is appreciated.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa/Mastercard accepted at mid-range venues and up.
- **Special note:** Money changers with "no commission" signs sometimes use sleight of hand. Use authorized (Bali Maspintjinra, Central Kuta) or bank-based changers.

## Vietnam
- **Currency:** Vietnamese dong (VND).
- **Cash vs card:** Cash king outside of major hotel chains and supermarkets. Large VND 500,000 notes can be hard to break.
- **ATMs:** Most charge VND 50,000–70,000 per foreign withdrawal.
- **Tipping:** Not traditional but appreciated. 5–10% at nicer restaurants; small amount for drivers and guides.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa/Mastercard accepted at hotels and chain restaurants; market stalls and smaller venues cash-only.

## Australia
- **Currency:** Australian dollar (AUD).
- **Cash vs card:** Highly card- and contactless-friendly. Many businesses no longer handle cash at all.
- **ATMs:** Common; Big 4 banks (Commonwealth, Westpac, ANZ, NAB) most reliable.
- **Tipping:** Not expected. Rounding up or 10% for excellent restaurant service.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa, Mastercard, and Amex near-universal. Surcharges (1–2%) sometimes applied.

## New Zealand
- **Currency:** New Zealand dollar (NZD).
- **Cash vs card:** Very card-friendly. Contactless universal.
- **ATMs:** Widespread.
- **Tipping:** Not customary.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard near-universal. Some venues surcharge Amex.

## Canada
- **Currency:** Canadian dollar (CAD).
- **Cash vs card:** Card-dominant; Interac debit is the local standard but foreign Visa/Mastercard work fine.
- **ATMs:** Plentiful.
- **Tipping:** 15–20% at sit-down restaurants, $1–2 per drink at bars, 10–15% for taxis and rideshares, $2–5 per night for housekeeping.
- **Card acceptance:** Contactless widely accepted.

## United Kingdom
- **Currency:** Pound sterling (GBP, £). Scottish and Northern Irish banknotes are legal tender in those regions but not always accepted in England.
- **Cash vs card:** Very card- and contactless-friendly. Many venues cashless.
- **ATMs:** "Cashpoints" common. Bank-branded machines usually free; some independent ATMs charge £1–2.
- **Tipping:** 10–12.5% at sit-down restaurants if service is not already added. Rounding up taxis. Not expected at pubs for table service (though a drink for the barkeep is common).
- **Card acceptance:** Visa/Mastercard universal; Amex common but not at every venue.

## Brazil
- **Currency:** Brazilian real (BRL, R$).
- **Cash vs card:** Card-dominant in cities; PIX (instant transfers via QR) is the de facto local payment method.
- **ATMs:** Plentiful; withdraw during daylight hours for safety.
- **Tipping:** 10% service typically added at restaurants. Extra tipping not expected.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa, Mastercard, and Elo widely accepted. Amex patchy.

## Portugal (Schengen Area)
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR, €).
- **Cash vs card:** Very card-friendly, including small cafes and pastelarias. Contactless universal.
- **ATMs:** Multibanco network is nationwide and reliable; very modest fees.
- **Tipping:** Not expected. Rounding up or leaving €1–2 for good service is polite; 5–10% at fine dining.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard universal; some venues accept only Multibanco (domestic debit) — check before ordering.

## Greece (Schengen Area)
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR, €).
- **Cash vs card:** Mixed. Cards widely accepted in Athens, Thessaloniki, and tourist islands; smaller tavernas and island stalls can still be cash-preferred.
- **ATMs:** Bank ATMs plentiful; some standalone machines charge higher fees — choose bank-branded where possible.
- **Tipping:** Not strictly expected. Rounding up the bill or leaving €1–2 per person at tavernas; 5–10% at fine dining.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard widely taken. Withdraw enough cash before ferry days on small islands.

## Ireland
- **Currency:** Euro (EUR, €) in the Republic of Ireland. Northern Ireland uses pound sterling (GBP, £).
- **Cash vs card:** Highly card- and contactless-friendly.
- **ATMs:** Plentiful. Bank-branded machines free; some independent operators charge small fees.
- **Tipping:** 10–12.5% at sit-down restaurants if service is not already added. Rounding up taxis. Not expected at pubs for table service.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard universal; Amex widely accepted.

## Switzerland (Schengen Area)
- **Currency:** Swiss franc (CHF). Switzerland is not in the Eurozone — many but not all businesses also accept EUR, usually at unfavorable rates.
- **Cash vs card:** Card-dominant. Contactless near-universal. Twint is the domestic mobile payment standard.
- **ATMs:** Plentiful; very modest fees.
- **Tipping:** Service is included. Rounding up for good service is standard; 5–10% only for exceptional dining.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard universal. Mountain railways and cable cars accept cards. Some small alpine huts still cash-only.
- **Special note:** Prices are among the highest in Europe; budget accordingly.

## Norway (Schengen Area)
- **Currency:** Norwegian krone (NOK). Norway is not in the Eurozone.
- **Cash vs card:** Extremely cashless — many businesses no longer accept cash at all. Contactless and mobile wallets are the default.
- **ATMs:** Plentiful but declining; many travelers make an entire trip without using any.
- **Tipping:** Not expected. Rounding up for good service.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard universal. Amex patchy. Bring a chip-and-PIN or contactless card — signature-only cards can fail at unattended fuel pumps and ticket machines.

## Czech Republic (Schengen Area)
- **Currency:** Czech koruna (CZK). The Czech Republic is not in the Eurozone.
- **Cash vs card:** Card-friendly in Prague and major cities; rural areas and some pubs still cash-preferred.
- **ATMs:** Bank ATMs (CSOB, KB, Ceská sporitelna) fair fees. Avoid Euronet standalone machines — they offer very poor exchange rates.
- **Tipping:** 10% at sit-down restaurants by telling the server the rounded total when paying; round up taxis.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard widely accepted.
- **Special note:** Decline dynamic currency conversion (DCC) at ATMs and card terminals; always pay in CZK.

## Argentina
- **Currency:** Argentine peso (ARS).
- **Cash vs card:** Card acceptance good in cities and hotels. Historically travelers exchanged USD informally ("blue dollar") for far better rates than the official one — recent reforms have narrowed the gap, so check current conditions close to travel.
- **ATMs:** Available but often with low per-withdrawal limits and high fees — bringing USD or EUR cash has historically given better value.
- **Tipping:** 10% at sit-down restaurants if service is not included. Rounding up for taxis.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard widely accepted. Some merchants offer better rates for cash.
- **Special note:** High inflation means prices shift rapidly; older price info online is often out of date.

## Morocco
- **Currency:** Moroccan dirham (MAD). Restricted currency — cannot be imported or exported in significant amounts.
- **Cash vs card:** Cash dominant in medinas, souks, and smaller towns. Hotels, larger restaurants, and chain stores take cards.
- **ATMs:** Plentiful in cities. Withdraw in MAD on arrival; keep exchange receipts for reconversion.
- **Tipping:** Widely expected but small. MAD 5–20 for restaurant service, hotel porters, and guided tours. Cafe service: round up.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard accepted at mid-to-upper-range venues.
- **Haggling:** Standard in souks — expect to negotiate 30–50% below the initial price. Not expected in fixed-price shops and supermarkets.

## Egypt
- **Currency:** Egyptian pound (EGP). Has undergone significant devaluation in recent years.
- **Cash vs card:** Cash useful everywhere; cards accepted at hotels, larger restaurants, and chain stores.
- **ATMs:** Plentiful in cities. USD is widely accepted informally at tourist sites and some hotels, often at poor rates — use it only for convenience, not as a primary option.
- **Tipping (baksheesh):** Widely expected and part of daily service culture. Small denominations (EGP 10–50) for porters, bathroom attendants, guides, and drivers. Restaurants: 10–15% on top of service charge.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard widely accepted in Cairo, Luxor, Aswan, and resort areas.
- **Special note:** Currency volatility means online prices can be stale — confirm close to travel.

## Philippines
- **Currency:** Philippine peso (PHP).
- **Cash vs card:** Cash preferred in markets, jeepneys, and smaller venues; cards accepted at malls, hotels, and chain restaurants.
- **ATMs:** Plentiful in Manila, Cebu, and major tourist hubs. Most charge PHP 250 per foreign withdrawal.
- **Tipping:** 10% service often added at restaurants — extra small tip appreciated. PHP 20–50 for porters and drivers.
- **Card acceptance:** Visa and Mastercard widely accepted at mid-range venues and up.
- **Special note:** GCash (domestic e-wallet) is ubiquitous among locals but usually requires a Philippine number to register.
