# Customs and Duty-Free Allowances by Destination

Use these notes as a planning guide, not a substitute for official customs authority guidance. Allowances and prohibited items change frequently and carry real penalties — always check the destination's customs website before departure.

- Allowances usually apply to travelers aged 17–18 or older; children's allowances are typically zero for alcohol and tobacco.
- Cash-declaration thresholds apply to the combined total carried by a traveler or family group in some jurisdictions.
- Counterfeit goods, protected wildlife products (ivory, coral, some shells), and certain foods are restricted almost everywhere.
- "Duty-free" applies to what you bring in; what you can *take out* of the origin country is a separate set of rules.

## France (Schengen Area)
- **Alcohol (non-EU arrivals):** 4L still wine + 16L beer + 1L spirits over 22% (or 2L of fortified wine/aperitif under 22%).
- **Tobacco (non-EU arrivals):** 200 cigarettes, 100 cigarillos, 50 cigars, or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** €430 for air/sea travelers, €300 by land, €150 for under-15s.
- **Cash declaration:** €10,000 or equivalent must be declared when entering or leaving the EU.
- **Prohibited:** Meat, dairy, and most animal products from non-EU countries. Counterfeit goods. Protected species and derivatives.
- **EU arrivals:** No duty-free limits, but "reasonable personal use" guidance applies (e.g. 800 cigarettes, 10L spirits, 90L wine).

## Japan
- **Alcohol:** 3 bottles (760ml each) total.
- **Tobacco:** 200 cigarettes or 50 cigars or 250g of tobacco. (Limits tightened after the end of dual-residence allowances.)
- **Perfume:** 2 ounces (about 60ml).
- **Other goods:** Up to ¥200,000 in total value, excluding items under ¥10,000.
- **Cash declaration:** Amounts over ¥1,000,000 must be declared.
- **Prohibited:** Narcotics, firearms, counterfeit items, certain stimulant drugs in common cold medicines (e.g. Vicks inhalers, Actifed), some ADHD medications. Fresh fruit, vegetables, meat, and dairy products are heavily restricted.
- **Special note:** The Visit Japan Web customs e-declaration speeds up arrival.

## United States
- **Alcohol:** 1L per traveler age 21+ duty-free; more may be allowed with duty (state limits also apply).
- **Tobacco:** 200 cigarettes and 100 cigars per traveler age 21+.
- **Other goods:** $800 personal exemption for US residents (returning), $200 for some short-trip situations. Non-residents: $100 gift exemption.
- **Cash declaration:** Amounts over $10,000 must be declared on FinCEN Form 105.
- **Prohibited:** Cuban cigars (relaxed rules have tightened again), most fresh fruit/vegetables/meat, Kinder Surprise eggs, absinthe with >10mg/kg thujone, counterfeit goods.
- **Special note:** Agricultural declaration is mandatory — failing to declare even apples or sandwiches carries fines.

## Thailand
- **Alcohol:** 1L per traveler.
- **Tobacco:** 200 cigarettes or 250g of cigars/tobacco. Exceeding carries heavy fines — even small over-imports have resulted in multi-thousand-baht penalties.
- **Other goods:** Personal items up to THB 20,000 in value.
- **Cash declaration:** Amounts over USD 20,000 equivalent must be declared.
- **Prohibited:** E-cigarettes, vaping devices and juice (illegal to import and possess). Fresh fruits and vegetables restricted. Buddha images larger than 5 inches may require an export permit when leaving.
- **Special note:** Vape possession can lead to arrest — do not bring them even for personal use.

## Spain (Schengen Area)
- **Alcohol (non-EU arrivals):** 4L still wine + 16L beer + 1L spirits over 22%.
- **Tobacco (non-EU arrivals):** 200 cigarettes or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** €430 air/sea, €300 land.
- **Cash declaration:** €10,000 entering or leaving the EU.
- **Prohibited:** Same EU list as France.

## UAE (Dubai)
- **Alcohol (non-Muslim travelers age 18+):** 4L of alcohol or 2 cartons (24 cans total) of beer, declared on arrival.
- **Tobacco:** 400 cigarettes, 50 cigars, 500g of tobacco.
- **Cash declaration:** Amounts over AED 60,000 (~USD 16,300) must be declared.
- **Prohibited:** Pork products, poppy seeds, narcotics (extreme penalties), certain medications (tramadol, codeine, CBD, some sleeping pills) without prior approval. Counterfeit goods. Offensive or pornographic materials. Items bearing Israeli markings are sometimes restricted.
- **Special note:** The Ministry of Health controlled medications list is strict — travelers on prescription opioids, stimulants, or benzodiazepines should apply for advance approval.

## Italy (Schengen Area)
- **Alcohol (non-EU arrivals):** 4L still wine + 16L beer + 1L spirits over 22%.
- **Tobacco (non-EU arrivals):** 200 cigarettes or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** €430 air/sea, €300 land.
- **Cash declaration:** €10,000 entering or leaving the EU.
- **Prohibited:** Standard EU restrictions; additional export controls on antiques and artworks over 50 years old.

## Germany (Schengen Area)
- **Alcohol (non-EU arrivals):** 4L still wine + 16L beer + 1L spirits.
- **Tobacco (non-EU arrivals):** 200 cigarettes or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** €430 air/sea, €300 land.
- **Cash declaration:** €10,000 entering or leaving the EU.
- **Prohibited:** Standard EU restrictions; pepper spray is legal in some forms but electric stun devices are not.

## Netherlands (Schengen Area)
- **Alcohol (non-EU arrivals):** 4L still wine + 16L beer + 1L spirits.
- **Tobacco (non-EU arrivals):** 200 cigarettes or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** €430 air/sea, €300 land.
- **Cash declaration:** €10,000 entering or leaving the EU.
- **Prohibited:** Standard EU restrictions; live bulbs and plants from outside the EU may require phytosanitary certificates even though the Netherlands is a major flower exporter.

## Singapore
- **Alcohol:** 1L spirits + 1L wine + 1L beer (or equivalent combinations) if coming from most countries, not arriving from Malaysia, and age 18+.
- **Tobacco:** No duty-free allowance. Every stick of tobacco must be declared and duty paid — even one pack.
- **Other goods:** GST relief up to SGD 500 for travelers age 18+ away for 48 hours or more; SGD 100 otherwise.
- **Cash declaration:** SGD 20,000 or equivalent.
- **Prohibited:** E-cigarettes and vaping devices (illegal to import or possess). Chewing gum (outside of therapeutic, declared forms). Narcotics (death penalty applies). Pornography, seditious materials.
- **Special note:** Enforcement is exceptionally strict. Declare all tobacco at the red channel.

## India
- **Alcohol:** 2L total for travelers age 21+.
- **Tobacco:** 100 cigarettes, 25 cigars, or 125g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** INR 50,000 for travelers age 10+ (INR 15,000 for under 10s and arrivals from Nepal/Bhutan/Myanmar by land).
- **Gold:** Male travelers can import up to 20g worth up to INR 50,000; female travelers up to 40g worth up to INR 100,000. Above this is dutiable.
- **Cash declaration:** Amounts over USD 5,000 in notes or USD 10,000 total (including traveler's cheques) must be declared.
- **Prohibited:** Satellite phones without license, drones without permit, e-cigarettes, certain pesticides, gold in bar/coin form above the passenger allowance.

## Indonesia (Bali)
- **Alcohol:** 1L per adult.
- **Tobacco:** 200 cigarettes, 25 cigars, or 100g of sliced tobacco.
- **Other goods:** USD 500 personal goods for travelers age 18+.
- **Cash declaration:** IDR 100,000,000 or equivalent.
- **Prohibited:** Narcotics (severe penalties, including death), pornography, certain wildlife products, counterfeit goods. E-cigarettes legal but taxed heavily.

## Vietnam
- **Alcohol:** 1.5L of spirits over 20%, or 2L under 20%, or 3L of beer.
- **Tobacco:** 200 cigarettes, 20 cigars, or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** VND 10,000,000 (~USD 400).
- **Cash declaration:** USD 5,000 or VND 15,000,000.
- **Prohibited:** Narcotics, weapons, pornography, antiques without permit, some political publications.

## Australia
- **Alcohol:** 2.25L per traveler age 18+.
- **Tobacco:** 25 cigarettes or 25g of other tobacco products per traveler age 18+ (one of the tightest in the world).
- **Other goods:** AUD 900 for adults, AUD 450 for under 18s.
- **Cash declaration:** AUD 10,000 or equivalent.
- **Prohibited:** Fresh food, meat, dairy, many seeds, wooden items without treatment certificate. Biosecurity fines are large — declare all food, plant, and animal items on the Incoming Passenger Card.

## New Zealand
- **Alcohol:** 3 bottles of spirits (up to 1.125L each) + 4.5L of wine/beer, age 17+.
- **Tobacco:** 50 cigarettes or 50g of cigars/tobacco.
- **Other goods:** NZD 700 for travelers age 17+.
- **Cash declaration:** NZD 10,000 or equivalent.
- **Prohibited:** Fresh food, plant material, unprocessed wood, honey, most seeds. Biosecurity enforcement is strict — NZD 400 instant fines for undeclared risk items.

## Canada
- **Alcohol:** 1.5L of wine or 1.14L of spirits or 24 × 355ml of beer/cider, age depends on province (18 or 19).
- **Tobacco:** 200 cigarettes + 50 cigars + 200g manufactured tobacco + 200 tobacco sticks, age 18+.
- **Other goods:** CAD 200 after 24 hours abroad, CAD 800 after 48 hours.
- **Cash declaration:** CAD 10,000 or equivalent.
- **Prohibited:** Firearms without permits, cannabis (despite legality inside Canada, cross-border import is federally prohibited), certain dairy, fresh produce, and plant material.

## United Kingdom
- **Alcohol (non-UK arrivals):** 42L beer + 18L still wine + 4L spirits over 22% (or 9L of sparkling/fortified under 22%).
- **Tobacco (non-UK arrivals):** 200 cigarettes or 100 cigarillos or 50 cigars or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** £390 for air/sea travelers, £270 if arriving in Northern Ireland from outside the EU.
- **Cash declaration:** £10,000 or equivalent.
- **Prohibited:** Meat and dairy from most non-EU countries, offensive weapons (butterfly knives, zombie knives), counterfeit goods, many plants without phytosanitary certificate.

## South Africa
- **Alcohol:** 2L of wine + 1L of spirits or liquor + 2L of beer/cider per traveler age 18+.
- **Tobacco:** 200 cigarettes + 20 cigars + 250g pipe tobacco.
- **Other goods:** ZAR 5,000 of new/used goods.
- **Cash declaration:** ZAR 25,000 or foreign currency equivalent of USD 10,000.
- **Prohibited:** Unprocessed ivory, rhino horn, certain plant and animal products, narcotics, counterfeit goods.

## Portugal (Schengen Area)
- **Alcohol (non-EU arrivals):** 4L still wine + 16L beer + 1L spirits over 22%.
- **Tobacco (non-EU arrivals):** 200 cigarettes or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** €430 air/sea, €300 land.
- **Cash declaration:** €10,000 entering or leaving the EU.
- **Prohibited:** Standard EU restrictions on meat, dairy, and counterfeit goods. Export of genuine Age of Discovery-era cultural artefacts requires permit.

## Greece (Schengen Area)
- **Alcohol (non-EU arrivals):** 4L still wine + 16L beer + 1L spirits over 22%.
- **Tobacco (non-EU arrivals):** 200 cigarettes or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** €430 air/sea, €300 land.
- **Cash declaration:** €10,000 entering or leaving the EU.
- **Prohibited:** Standard EU restrictions. Export of antiquities — including beach pebbles from some archaeological sites and any object over 100 years old — is tightly controlled.

## Ireland
- **Alcohol (non-EU arrivals):** 4L still wine + 16L beer + 1L spirits over 22% (or 2L of fortified wine/aperitif under 22%).
- **Tobacco (non-EU arrivals):** 200 cigarettes or 100 cigarillos or 50 cigars or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** €430 for air/sea travelers, €300 by land, €215 for under-15s.
- **Cash declaration:** €10,000 or equivalent when entering or leaving the EU.
- **Prohibited:** Most meat and dairy from outside the EU, counterfeit goods, weapons including flick knives and knuckle dusters. Strict biosecurity on plants, seeds, and bulbs.
- **EU arrivals:** No duty-free limits within the EU; reasonable-personal-use guidance applies.

## Switzerland (Schengen Area)
- **Alcohol:** 5L up to 18% ABV + 1L over 18% ABV per traveler age 17+.
- **Tobacco:** 250 cigarettes/cigars or 250g of other tobacco per traveler age 17+.
- **Other goods:** CHF 300 total value for personal goods; above that VAT applies.
- **Cash declaration:** No declaration threshold but random checks occur; amounts over CHF 10,000 may attract questions.
- **Prohibited:** Meat and dairy imports from non-EU countries are restricted (Switzerland is not in the EU customs union), counterfeit goods, certain medications without prescription. Alpine protected plant and mineral specimens cannot be taken from nature reserves.
- **Special note:** Switzerland has its own customs rules separate from the EU despite Schengen membership — duty-free allowances are significantly lower than neighboring EU countries.

## Norway (Schengen Area)
- **Alcohol:** Complex quota based on types. Example: 1L spirits + 1.5L wine + 2L beer, age 20+ for spirits/18+ for wine/beer. Excess can be declared and duty paid.
- **Tobacco:** 200 cigarettes + 100 cigars + 250g of other tobacco, age 18+.
- **Other goods:** NOK 6,000 for trips under 24 hours, NOK 3,000 otherwise.
- **Cash declaration:** NOK 25,000 or equivalent must be declared.
- **Prohibited:** Snus for personal use allowed but strict quantity limits. Potatoes and fresh meat from outside the EEA are restricted. Firearms require permits.
- **Special note:** Norwegian alcohol duties are very high; many travelers intentionally import their full allowance via the Tax-Free at Oslo arrivals.

## Czech Republic (Schengen Area)
- **Alcohol (non-EU arrivals):** 4L still wine + 16L beer + 1L spirits over 22%.
- **Tobacco (non-EU arrivals):** 200 cigarettes or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** €430 air/sea, €300 land.
- **Cash declaration:** €10,000 entering or leaving the EU.
- **Prohibited:** Standard EU restrictions. Bohemian cut crystal and antique glass over 50 years old may require export permit from the Ministry of Culture.

## Argentina
- **Alcohol:** 2L per traveler age 18+.
- **Tobacco:** 400 cigarettes + 50 cigars + 500g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** USD 500 in personal goods + additional USD 500 at duty-free shops on arrival.
- **Cash declaration:** USD 10,000 or equivalent.
- **Prohibited:** Firearms without permit, narcotics, fresh food from some regions, certain electronics without declaration over the allowance.
- **Special note:** Argentina sometimes applies additional declaration rules during economic controls — check the latest AFIP (customs) guidance close to travel.

## Morocco
- **Alcohol:** 1L of spirits + 1 bottle of wine per traveler age 18+.
- **Tobacco:** 200 cigarettes, 25 cigars, or 250g of tobacco.
- **Currency:** Moroccan dirhams cannot be imported or exported; exchange at authorized bureaus on arrival and reconvert before departure (keep receipts).
- **Other goods:** MAD 2,000 in personal goods.
- **Cash declaration:** Foreign currency over MAD 100,000 equivalent must be declared.
- **Prohibited:** Drones without advance permit (often confiscated on arrival), certain satellite phones, pornography, Christian religious materials for proselytization.

## Egypt
- **Alcohol:** 3L duty-free at the airport on arrival; wider allowances possible through duty-free shops within 48 hours of arrival.
- **Tobacco:** 200 cigarettes, 25 cigars, or 200g of tobacco.
- **Currency:** Egyptian pounds are restricted — do not import or export significant amounts. Declare foreign currency over USD 10,000 on arrival.
- **Prohibited:** Drones (almost always confiscated), satellite phones without license, certain over-the-counter medicines containing codeine, narcotics (heavy penalties). Antiquities cannot be exported without a Ministry of Antiquities permit — genuine or not.
- **Special note:** Egyptian customs can be assertive about undeclared electronics; keep receipts for new laptops or cameras.

## Philippines
- **Alcohol:** 2L per traveler age 18+.
- **Tobacco:** 400 cigarettes, 50 cigars, or 250g of tobacco.
- **Other goods:** PHP 10,000 (~USD 175) in personal goods.
- **Cash declaration:** PHP 50,000 or USD 10,000 equivalent.
- **Prohibited:** Firearms without permit, narcotics, counterfeit goods, certain plants and wildlife products. E-cigarettes legal but taxed.
- **Special note:** eTravel declaration (including customs) is required for arrivals — fill in online within 72 hours of travel.
